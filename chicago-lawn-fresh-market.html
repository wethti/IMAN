Mission

IMAN Fresh Market: Chicago Lawn provides affordable, dignified grocery retail and wraparound food-security programs for Chicago Lawn residents, as a holistic approach to health equity, community healing, and neighborhood economic development on the South Side.

Modeled on IMAN's Go Green Community Fresh Market in Englewood — four years operating, 9,000+ members, and a replicable proof-of-concept for what a nonprofit grocery store can do in a disinvested neighborhood.

The Problem

Chicago Lawn
is a food
desert.

Chicago Lawn is among Chicago's most disinvested South Side neighborhoods. 60% of residents live at or below the federal poverty line — yet the neighborhood lacks a full-service, affordable grocery store within reasonable distance.

Without access to fresh food, residents face elevated rates of cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and obesity — the same diet-related chronic conditions that drive the 30-year life expectancy gap between Englewood, Chicago Lawn, and high-income Chicago neighborhoods.

The Fresh Market is not a standalone store. It is one node in IMAN's integrated community ecosystem — designed to connect every resident to health, jobs, housing, and healing.

Health Center Green ReEntry Supportive Housing CHW Team
01
Food-Is-Medicine · IMAN Health Center
Patients screened for food insecurity at IMAN's FQHC are referred directly to the Fresh Market and enrolled in Fresh Boost credit. Community Health Workers operate at every distribution day — providing blood pressure screenings, SNAP enrollment support, and nutrition education.

All Fresh Market staff earn living wages. The planned Culinary track in Green ReEntry creates a direct pipeline: participants train in food service, graduate with certifications, and step into roles at the store — connecting workforce development to food access in one corridor.

Building on the model of Griot Plaza at 63rd & Racine — the Fresh Market site becomes a neighborhood anchor with programming, public gathering space, and IMAN's community engagement team embedded for safety and outreach.

Signature Program

Fresh Boost:
Dignified
Food Access

Instead of directing food-insecure households to a pantry, Fresh Boost provides a monthly in-store credit that participants spend freely — on whatever they need, whenever they need it. No charity counter. No judgment. Just a full grocery store, available like it is for everyone else.

Developed with the Greater Chicago Food Depository, Fresh Boost is positioned as a Medicaid food-is-medicine model aligned with emerging federal and state food-as-medicine policy.

IMAN FQHC screens patient for food insecurity
Patient enrolled in Fresh Boost — receives monthly store credit
Participant shops freely at Fresh Market, preserving dignity and choice

About IMAN
Fostering
Health, Wellness
& Healing

IMAN — the Inner-City Muslim Action Network — uses the arts, civic engagement, and direct service to foster health, wellness, and healing in communities fractured by poverty and violence.

Founded in 1997 on Chicago's South Side by Rami Nashashibi, IMAN has grown from a community organizing group into a multi-program institution operating a Federally Qualified Health Center, Green ReEntry workforce development, the Go Green Community Fresh Market, Griot Plaza, and supportive housing — all within a tight geographic corridor in Englewood and Chicago Lawn.

IMAN's thesis is holistic: durable impact requires addressing health, food, housing, employment, and community simultaneously — through trusted local institutions that have earned their place over decades of authentic relationship.
