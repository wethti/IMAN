# Evidence Base: Recreation & Community

Gap estimates in action_list.md are derived from the following studies.

---

**Muthukumar P / Branas CC et al. — Citywide cluster-randomized trial to restore blighted vacant land (PNAS)**
The strongest direct evidence in this domain. Greening and maintaining vacant lots produced a 29% reduction in gun violence in surrounding blocks. Estimated impact if scaled citywide: over 350 fewer shootings per year. Also reduced resident stress and fear. Supports ~0.5 yr estimate for vacant lot greening.

**Twohig-Bennett C & Jones A — The health benefits of the great outdoors: a systematic review and meta-analysis (2018)**
Meta-analysis of 103 observational studies found that exposure to greenspace was associated with reduced all-cause mortality, cardiovascular mortality, type 2 diabetes, and mental health conditions. Greenspace access is both a direct health pathway and a proxy for neighborhood investment and safety. Supports ~0.3–0.5 yr estimates for plaza, park, and landscape investments.

**Katherine Perez et al. — Environmental and health effects of the Barcelona superblocks**
Barcelona's superblock program (restricting car traffic, creating pedestrian plazas and greenspace) was modeled to prevent approximately 665 premature deaths per year in the city and increase average life expectancy by 0.20 years. Also produced significant reductions in air pollution and noise. The closest real-world analog to the Griot Plaza / community corridor model.

**NEJMoa — A Cluster-Randomized Trial of Blood-Pressure Reduction in Black Barbershops (2018)**
Trusted community venues (barbershops) achieved sustained BP control in 63.6% of participants versus 11.7% in controls — a massive clinical difference. Confirms the principle that health outcomes improve dramatically when services are delivered in trusted community spaces rather than clinical settings. Supports the community node → health outcome chain.

**Vartiainen E — The North Karelia Project: Cardiovascular Disease Prevention in Finland**
North Karelia, a high-CVD region of Finland, achieved an 80% reduction in cardiovascular mortality over 30 years through community-wide behavior change programs, including physical activity promotion and dietary shifts. The community engagement and public space infrastructure was essential to reaching population-level behavior change. Confirms that sustained community programming can achieve very large life expectancy gains at neighborhood scale.

**chicago_life_expectancy_foundation.md — Healthy homes and environmental bundle**
The project's synthesis estimates the combined built-environment and environmental remediation bundle contributes 0.3–1.0 years of life expectancy gain over the medium term.
