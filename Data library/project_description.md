# IMAN Knowledge Map: Project Description

## Overview

This project is a structured research and strategic planning system built to support the **Inner-City Muslim Action Network (IMAN)** - a Chicago-based community development organization founded in 1997 - in its mission to close a 30-year life expectancy gap between disinvested South Side neighborhoods (primarily Englewood and Chicago Lawn) and affluent areas like Streeterville.

The Knowledge Map systematizes evidence-based research across nine domains of neighborhood health, translating peer-reviewed studies into actionable interventions with quantified life expectancy impact estimates and cost ranges. Its ultimate purpose is to help IMAN prioritize initiatives, build its 2040 strategic plan, and make the case to funders for the long-term population health impact of its integrated approach.

The goal behind this project is to create a system to accelerate the process of evaluating, selecting, and executing on ideas, allowing researchers to connect the dots between the existing studies and new ideas in seconds.
