## ED1 — Early Childhood Education (~2.0 yr LE gain) [Source: decision_tree.md]

**Causal mechanism.** Intensive ECE (Perry, Abecedarian, Chicago CPC) produces long-run reductions in chronic disease, substance use, and criminal involvement among participants, with Perry producing a $7–12 social ROI per $1 invested [Heckman, evidence_base.md Education]. Pathway: cognitive + self-regulation development → educational attainment → income + health behaviors → LE. Chicago CPC is the most directly transferable precedent: statistically significant reductions in depressive symptoms, substance abuse, and health risk behaviors at age 37 [Varshney et al., evidence_base.md Education].

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** (a) Kindergarten readiness (Illinois KIDS) in Englewood. (b) 3rd-grade reading proficiency (CPS). (c) ECE enrollment rate among eligible Englewood 3-4 year-olds.

**Identification strategy.** Borrowed long-run RCT effect sizes from Perry and CPC 30+ year follow-ups, scaled to IMAN's incremental enrollment volume. For short-run evaluation, DiD on kindergarten-readiness across community areas by ECE-slot availability.

**Minimum detectable effect.** KIDS readiness shifts of 3-5 percentage points are detectable at community-area scale.

**Threats to validity.** Selection into ECE (more motivated parents); low program fidelity at scale vs. Perry's intensive original design.

**Show the investor:** Heckman ROI benchmark scaled to IMAN-supported slots in Englewood.
**Show the client:** annual KIDS kindergarten-readiness gap vs. Chicago median, with ECE-slot coverage overlay.

## ED2 — Nurse-Family Partnership (~1.5 yr LE gain) [Source: decision_tree.md]

**Causal mechanism.** NFP's RCT evidence (Olds et al.) shows 50% reduction in child abuse/neglect, better maternal/child health through age 19, and long-run participant improvements in employment and lower substance use [evidence_base.md Education]. Pathway: prenatal + early home visits → improved maternal health behaviors + infant care + stress reduction → birth outcomes + developmental trajectory → LE.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** (a) Tract-level preterm birth and LBW rate (IDPH). (b) Infant mortality rate (IDPH). (c) CPS-reported maltreatment rate. (d) NFP enrollment rate among eligible first-time mothers.

**Identification strategy.** Borrowed RCT effect size from Olds' NFP trials scaled to enrollment volume. DiD on birth-outcome trajectories if NFP is expanded in a defined catchment.

**Minimum detectable effect.** LBW ~10-12% baseline; 1.5-point reduction detectable with ~2,000 births.

**Threats to validity.** Implementation fidelity (nurse caseload, visit count); selection of enrolled mothers.

**Show the investor:** NFP RCT-derived mortality-averted per enrolled mother × IMAN enrollment volume.
**Show the client:** quarterly NFP enrollment and retention dashboard tied to CHW outreach metrics.

## ED3 — School Funding Equity (~1.0 yr LE gain) [Source: decision_tree.md]

**Causal mechanism.** Jackson et al. show a 10% per-pupil spending increase raises adult wages, reduces poverty, and cuts incarceration, with largest effects for low-income children [evidence_base.md Education]. Pathway: school resources → educational attainment → income + incarceration → LE.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** (a) CPS Englewood per-pupil spending ratio to district median. (b) 11th-grade SAT/PSAT achievement. (c) High-school graduation rate.

**Identification strategy.** Borrowed Jackson et al. effect size applied to funding-gap closure. Advocacy/policy line — not a direct IMAN program.

**Minimum detectable effect.** N/A — borrowed effect framework.

**Threats to validity.** Implementation: dollars don't automatically become quality. Policy volatility.

**Show the investor:** per-pupil-dollar to adult-earnings elasticity from Jackson, applied to the Englewood-CPS funding gap.
**Show the client:** policy memo with quantified projected impact; no IMAN operational lift.

## ED4 — Teacher Quality Investment (~0.8 yr LE gain) [Source: decision_tree.md]

**Causal mechanism.** Chetty et al. Teachers II: 1 SD higher teacher value-added raises per-student NPV earnings by ~$40,000 and reduces incarceration [evidence_base.md Education]. Pathway: teacher effectiveness → student attainment → adult earnings/incarceration → LE.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** (a) Teacher value-added distribution in Englewood-zoned CPS schools. (b) Teacher retention rate. (c) Student test-score growth.

**Identification strategy.** Borrowed Chetty effect size. Policy/advocacy line.

**Minimum detectable effect.** N/A directly.

**Threats to validity.** Value-added measurement error; teacher churn.

**Show the investor:** VA-to-earnings elasticity applied to Englewood's teacher-quality gap.
**Show the client:** advocacy memo for CPS on teacher-quality investment in neighborhood schools.

## ED5 — Neighborhood Poverty Reduction (~1.5 yr LE gain) [Source: decision_tree.md]

**Causal mechanism.** MTO / Chetty: children exposed to lower-poverty neighborhoods earn 31% more as adults and have better health [evidence_base.md Education/Housing]. One Summer Plus RCT: 43% reduction in violent-crime arrests among summer-employed youth [Heller S, evidence_base.md Education]. Pathway: reduced violence exposure + improved peer networks + better schools + employment → life trajectory → LE.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** (a) Community-area poverty rate. (b) Violent-crime arrest rate among 14-24yo. (c) Opportunity-Atlas upward-mobility index.

**Identification strategy.** Synthetic control on Englewood upward-mobility trajectory. Borrowed RCT from Heller for summer-jobs component.

**Minimum detectable effect.** Poverty rates shift slowly; 3-percentage-point detectable at 10-year scale.

**Threats to validity.** Multi-decade horizon; many concurrent interventions.

**Show the investor:** Heller summer-jobs RCT ATE scaled to Englewood youth population.
**Show the client:** Opportunity-Atlas upward-mobility benchmark with annual CPS employment-program linkage.

## Summary for A4

Five ED initiatives. ED1 and ED2 have the strongest RCT precedents. ED3–ED4 are policy/advocacy lines. ED5 is best framed as a composite outcome that many IMAN bundles contribute to simultaneously.
