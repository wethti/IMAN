# A9 — Funding Mechanism Evaluation

Cross-ref `A0_cross_cutting_methodology.md`. Claims logged in `verification/claims_stream_A.csv`.

**Framing note (from evidence_base.md Funding):** funding mechanisms do not directly reduce the LE gap — they enable the programs that do. Evaluation therefore measures (a) throughput (dollars deployed per year to IMAN or IMAN-like programs), (b) alignment (match between dollars and highest-evidence pathways), (c) durability (multi-year commitment vs. one-time), and (d) attributable downstream proxy outcomes via the programs the dollars finance.

No per-initiative LE estimates appear in the decision tree for FU1-FU5 [DATA GAP — by design].

---

## FU1 — NMTC / HUD Choice Neighborhoods (no LE estimate in tree [DATA GAP])

**Causal mechanism.** NMTC is the primary financing mechanism for the Health Center Expansion (capital stack complete, awaiting approval [IMAN_initiatives_profile.md]); HUD Choice Neighborhoods is the federal program for neighborhood-scale revitalization plans. Pathway: capital unlocks → enables IM1 and IM2 → downstream LE effects through HC1 and HO1.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** Dollars deployed, project completion on schedule, downstream program-level proxy outcomes (inherited from A1, A3, A8).

**Identification strategy.** No independent causal claim for the funding mechanism; attribution is to the financed program. Case-study benchmarking against Choice Neighborhoods peer sites (e.g., Boston Roxbury, New Orleans Iberville).

**Minimum detectable effect.** N/A at funding-mechanism level.

**Threats to validity.** Policy and program risk (NMTC allocation uncertainty, HUD-program changes).

**Show the investor:** leverage ratio (grant dollars to total project capital), NMTC timeline risk, Choice Neighborhoods peer benchmarks.
**Show the client:** capital-stack status tracker and contingency risk matrix.

## FU2 — CDFI / Social Impact Bonds (no LE estimate in tree [DATA GAP])

**Causal mechanism.** CDFI (Community Development Financial Institutions) lending and SIBs (pay-for-success) create multi-year, outcomes-aligned financing pools. Pathway: durable financing → program stability → sustained proxy-outcome improvement. Manitoba Mincome evidence [Forget EL 2011, evidence_base.md Funding]: sustained income security reduced hospitalizations 8.5%. Shows the value of *durable* over one-time funding.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** Multi-year committed dollars, SIB performance thresholds hit vs. missed.

**Identification strategy.** Case-study benchmarking against published SIB evaluations (Massachusetts Juvenile Justice, Utah Preschool).

**Minimum detectable effect.** N/A.

**Threats to validity.** SIB structures can misalign incentives (focus on short-term, easily-measured outcomes).

**Show the investor:** SIB outcome-payer framework — how IMAN proxy outcomes could be structured as SIB payment triggers.
**Show the client:** CDFI lender pipeline with IMAN-aligned capital availability.

---

## FU3 — SNAP / WIC / EITC Programs (no LE estimate in tree [DATA GAP])

**Causal mechanism.** These programs each have their own evaluation row elsewhere (FA1 SNAP, FA2 WIC, FA5 EITC). As funding mechanisms, the evaluation question is policy-advocacy: preserving and expanding public benefit programs that IMAN channels residents into. Bolsa Familia evidence [Rasella et al., evidence_base.md Funding]: 17% reduction in child mortality, 58% in nutrition-related child mortality — a large-N demonstration that sustained public-benefit financing moves population mortality.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** Englewood/Chicago Lawn benefit enrollment shares, total benefit dollars flowing into the catchment per year.

**Identification strategy.** Borrowed effect sizes from program-specific evaluations. Advocacy-impact tracking for IMAN's coalition work with the Greater Chicago Food Depository [IMAN_initiatives_profile.md].

**Minimum detectable effect.** N/A directly; borrowed from A2.

**Threats to validity.** Federal policy volatility; state budget shifts pushing SNAP cost-share risk onto Illinois.

**Show the investor:** public-benefit dollars per Englewood resident as a social-determinant-of-health investment, benchmarked against Bolsa Familia evidence.
**Show the client:** annual enrollment-share dashboard and advocacy-campaign ROI tracker.

## FU4 — Philanthropy + Foundation Grants (no LE estimate in tree [DATA GAP])

**Causal mechanism.** Multi-year foundation commitments (Crown Family Philanthropy and similar [IMAN_initiatives_profile.md]) are the flexible, fast-moving capital that subsidizes Fresh Market below-cost staples, CHW team expansion, and pilot programs before they are eligible for public funding. Pathway: flexible private capital → pilot programs → evidence generation → public funding → scale.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** Multi-year philanthropic-commitment dollar volume, pilot-to-scale conversion rate, grant renewal rate.

**Identification strategy.** Case-study; no independent causal claim.

**Minimum detectable effect.** N/A.

**Threats to validity.** Philanthropic fashion cycles; donor concentration risk.

**Show the investor:** pilot-to-scale conversion track record; Crown Family multi-year commitment as benchmark.
**Show the client:** funder-pipeline dashboard with restricted-vs-unrestricted allocation tracker.

---

## FU5 — City & State Capital Grants (no LE estimate in tree [DATA GAP])

**Causal mechanism.** City (CDPH for CHW team funding) and state (IDPH, DCEO capital) grants are the stable backbone of IMAN's public-sector support. Pathway: stable public funding → program durability → sustained proxy-outcome improvement.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** City + state dollars per year, renewal rates, cost-share leverage.

**Identification strategy.** Case-study; throughput tracking.

**Minimum detectable effect.** N/A.

**Threats to validity.** State budget cycles; administration changes.

**Show the investor:** public-funding base as downside-protection for the IMAN program portfolio.
**Show the client:** city + state grant-renewal timeline dashboard.

## Summary for A9

Five funding-mechanism rows. None carry independent LE-gain estimates by construction (they enable the programs that do). Evaluation framing: throughput, alignment, durability, and attributable downstream program outcomes. The strongest investor narrative here is the durability-and-leverage story (Bolsa Familia, Mincome, long-run Chetty income-LE) — that sustained, flexible, multi-year capital is what converts program presence into population-mortality movement.
