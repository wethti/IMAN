## CP1 — Group Violence Intervention (~1.0 yr LE gain) [Source: decision_tree.md]

**Causal mechanism.** Braga & Weisburd meta-analysis: GVI / focused deterrence reduces homicide and violent crime 30–60% in target areas [evidence_base.md Other city projects]. Oakland Ceasefire: 30% homicide reduction over three years [Barao 2019]. Pathway: direct mortality reduction in the highest-risk young-adult-male population, which drives the LE gap disproportionately through YPLL-75.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** (a) Monthly homicide and non-fatal shooting rate in CA 68/66 (Chicago Data Portal + Cook County ME). (b) YPLL-75 contribution of homicide (CDC WONDER). (c) Callback list retention and custom-notification completion rate (program log).

**Identification strategy.** Borrowed meta-analytic effect size (Braga & Weisburd). ITS on GVI launch. Synthetic control on peer Chicago community areas. Oakland and Boston as benchmark comparisons.

**Minimum detectable effect.** Homicide rate reduction of 20%+ detectable with 3 years of monthly data given Englewood's elevated baseline.

**Threats to validity.** Regression to mean on peak-violence years; displacement to adjacent tracts (measure with buffer zones); implementation fidelity.

**Show the investor:** lives-saved projection at published GVI elasticity × IMAN's catchment baseline homicide rate.
**Show the client:** monthly shootings dashboard with GVI contact-list coverage overlay.

## CP2 — Cure Violence / READI (~1.5 yr LE gain) [Source: decision_tree.md]

**Causal mechanism.** Cure Violence shooting reductions 30–60% in fidelity sites [Ransford, evidence_base.md Other city projects]; Baltimore 56% homicide reduction in one district. READI Chicago: 79% reduction in violent-crime involvement among highest-risk men combining transitional employment + CBT [READI eval Oct 2023, evidence_base.md]. Pathway: direct interruption of retaliation cycles + behavioral change in highest-risk individuals → mortality.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** (a) Monthly shootings in coverage area. (b) Conflict-mediation counts (program log). (c) 18-month participant outcomes: arrests, employment, earnings (IDOC, IDES).

**Identification strategy.** READI has a published RCT — borrow directly. For Cure Violence, use the Ransford meta-analytic effect size and ITS on deployment. Synthetic-control against peer Chicago CAs that did not deploy.

**Minimum detectable effect.** Participant-level employment/arrest outcomes detectable with N=200 per arm (well within READI-scale); community-level shootings require 3+ years.

**Threats to validity.** Implementation fidelity is the single largest moderator in the Cure Violence literature. Contamination from concurrent GVI. Staff safety and turnover.

**Show the investor:** READI RCT ATE scaled to IMAN catchment participants — this is the single strongest effect size in the portfolio.
**Show the client:** participant-flow dashboard: recruitment → 18-month retention → arrest/earnings outcomes vs. non-participant matched peers.

## CP3 — Summer Youth Employment (~0.6 yr LE gain) [Source: decision_tree.md]

**Causal mechanism.** Heller One Summer Plus RCT: 43% reduction in violent-crime arrests among participating youth [Science, evidence_base.md Other city projects]. Pathway: situational displacement + identity shift + wage income → reduced violence exposure → mortality.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** (a) Violent-crime arrest rate among 14-24yo program participants at 12 and 24 months (CPD). (b) School attendance and graduation rates. (c) Post-program employment.

**Identification strategy.** Direct replication of Heller RCT effect size. Wait-list randomization feasible if IMAN over-subscribes summer slots.

**Minimum detectable effect.** 20-percentage-point arrest-rate reduction detectable with N=300 per arm.

**Threats to validity.** Selection of motivated applicants; program-slot supply constraints.

**Show the investor:** direct Heller RCT replication in Englewood youth cohort — a Chicago-specific, already-published result.
**Show the client:** summer-cohort outcome tracker with pre/post arrest + school-engagement metrics.

## CP4 — Naloxone Saturation (~0.7 yr LE gain) [Source: decision_tree.md]

**Causal mechanism.** Walley et al. BMJ 2013: Massachusetts communities with high naloxone distribution had 46% lower overdose mortality [evidence_base.md Other city projects]. Pathway: bystander reversal of opioid overdoses.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** (a) Monthly overdose mortality per 100,000 (Cook County ME). (b) Non-fatal EMS overdose runs with naloxone administration. (c) Naloxone kits distributed per 1,000 residents.

**Identification strategy.** ITS on monthly overdose mortality around saturation launch. DiD vs. non-saturation Chicago CAs. Borrowed Walley effect size.

**Minimum detectable effect.** 20% overdose-mortality reduction detectable with 2-3 years of monthly data.

**Threats to validity.** Fentanyl supply shocks dominate trend; subtract national/regional baseline. Kit distribution does not equal kit use.

**Show the investor:** Walley elasticity × saturation coverage = projected overdose-mortality reduction.
**Show the client:** naloxone-distribution dashboard with EMS-run overlap.

## Summary for A7

Five CP initiatives. CP2 (READI/Cure Violence) and CP3 (summer youth) have the strongest individual-level RCT foundations and are IMAN's clearest gap-filling opportunities. CP1 is a well-evidenced city-scale policy that IMAN can join as a partner. CP4 is strong community-level ITS design. CP5 is the A6 RC1 design in a city-project framing.
