# A8 — IMAN Initiatives Evaluation

Cross-ref `A0_cross_cutting_methodology.md`. Claims logged in `verification/claims_stream_A.csv`.

These are IMAN's own operating programs. Evaluation emphasis is on data IMAN already collects or could easily collect.

---

## IM1 — Health Center Expansion (~1.5 yr LE gain) [Source: decision_tree.md]

**Causal mechanism.** Expanding IMAN's FQHC from 3,500 sq ft to ~30,000 sq ft with integrated behavioral health [IMAN_initiatives_profile.md] multiplies panel capacity, enables same-visit warm handoffs, and adds rooftop green space + conference rooms as health-promoting settings. See A1 HC1 for the full causal frame. Key IMAN-specific angle: Northwestern Memorial co-investment rationale is shared-value (reducing downstream hospital utilization).

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** UDS measures (BP control, HbA1c, depression remission), ACSC ED-rate (IHA), panel-size growth, Northwestern claims-based utilization for shared patients.

**Identification strategy.** Pre/post the 2026+ groundbreak with DiD vs. comparison FQHCs that did not expand. UDS panel benchmarks are the ready-to-use comparison pool.

**Minimum detectable effect.** 5-point panel BP-control improvement; 10% ACSC-ED reduction.

**Threats to validity.** Panel composition shift (expansion draws sicker patients); pandemic era history.

**Show the investor:** per-dollar healthcare-cost avoidance for Northwestern + per-patient UDS quality improvement.
**Show the client:** UDS + claims-share dashboard tracking the shared-value thesis.

## IM2 — Regenerator Housing (~1.0 yr LE gain) [Source: decision_tree.md]

**Causal mechanism.** ~48 supportive housing units in a converted school building, with ground-floor pharmacy + FQHC + integration with Green Reentry [IMAN_initiatives_profile.md]. See A3 HO1 for the full causal frame. IMAN-specific: the Regenerator is explicitly designed as a bundle site (housing + health + workforce in one parcel).

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** Resident retention, ED/inpatient pre/post, employment/earnings, recidivism.

**Identification strategy.** Within-person pre/post claims-based utilization. Propensity match to Green Reentry non-housed cohort.

**Minimum detectable effect.** 25% ED-visit reduction per resident-year.

**Threats to validity.** Selection by IMAN into housing; bundle entanglement.

**Show the investor:** per-resident healthcare-utilization delta; Regenerator as flagship of the bundle thesis.
**Show the client:** resident cohort dashboard: housing retention × employment × health utilization.

## IM3 — Green Reentry Workforce (~0.8 yr LE gain) [Source: decision_tree.md]

**Causal mechanism.** Workforce training in 5+ trades (expanding to 8) [IMAN_initiatives_profile.md], with 91% retention and 350 2024 graduates. Income → LE: Chetty $10k/yr ≈ 1 LE-year [evidence_base.md Funding]. Pathway: living-wage employment + social stabilization + reduced recidivism → mortality.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** (a) Post-program earnings (IDES UI wages). (b) 3-year recidivism rate (IDOC). (c) Housing stability and health-utilization (for subset enrolled in IMAN housing + FQHC). (d) Cohort 91% retention → benchmarked against reentry-program national medians.

**Identification strategy.** Individual-level pre/post earnings and recidivism with propensity-matched non-participant reentry population. Borrowed effect size from READI-adjacent literature. Staggered-cohort DiD as trade additions roll out.

**Minimum detectable effect.** 30-percentage-point recidivism gap; $5k/yr earnings gap — both detectable at IMAN's cohort scale.

**Threats to validity.** Selection (IMAN screens applicants); IDOC data match completeness; concurrent housing+FQHC effects.

**Show the investor:** dollars-earned per graduate × Chetty income-LE coefficient, with recidivism-avoidance cost savings.
**Show the client:** cohort-level dashboard: graduation → placement → earnings trajectory → recidivism.

## IM4 — Fresh Market Operations (~0.8 yr LE gain) [Source: decision_tree.md]

**Causal mechanism.** See A2 FA3 for full causal frame. IMAN-specific data: 20% EBT transaction share, 23% SNAP revenue share, 9,000+ loyalty members, 9-10% subsidized sales share, 25+ BIPOC vendors [IMAN_initiatives_profile.md]. Pathway: food access + affordability → diet → cardiometabolic outcomes → LE.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** PLACES F&V consumption and obesity/diabetes prevalence; Fresh Market basket-composition shift; repeat-visit frequency; per-capita fresh-food spend.

**Identification strategy.** Synthetic control on Englewood catchment vs. peer food-desert tracts (see A0 §3). Dose-response on distance-to-Fresh-Market for block-level outcomes.

**Minimum detectable effect.** See A2 FA3; wide CIs expected for cardiometabolic proxies at single-catchment scale.

**Threats to validity.** Bundle entanglement with Griot Plaza and Regenerator.

**Show the investor:** Fresh Market synthetic-control chart — the single most investor-recognizable IMAN visual.
**Show the client:** store-operations dashboard (EBT share, basket size, subsidized-item volume) with FQHC-patient clinical outcome linkage.

## IM5 — Weekend Warriors Youth (~0.5 yr LE gain) [Source: decision_tree.md]

**Causal mechanism.** Summer/weekend youth engagement through Green Reentry infrastructure [IMAN_initiatives_profile.md]. See A7 CP3 for the One Summer Plus RCT frame (Heller 43% violent-crime arrest reduction).

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** Participant arrest rate, school attendance, post-program employment or education status.

**Identification strategy.** Borrowed Heller RCT effect size. Wait-list randomization feasible as program scales.

**Minimum detectable effect.** With small current cohort size, rely on borrowed effect; build in-house randomization capacity as Weekend Warriors grows.

**Threats to validity.** Program still in early stages per IMAN_initiatives_profile.md; fidelity vs. One Summer Plus protocol to be assessed.

**Show the investor:** Heller ATE scaled to program enrollment.
**Show the client:** cohort-level engagement and outcome dashboard.

## IM6 — IMAN Oasis Café + Community (no LE estimate in tree [DATA GAP])

**Causal mechanism.** Renovation of IMAN HQ building at 63rd/California as community-facing café + courtyard + conference + arts + meditation/prayer spaces [IMAN_initiatives_profile.md]. Parallels RC2 Griot Plaza in function (community concentration node). No direct RCT exists for this specific package; evidence base is social-cohesion and third-place literature, plus Branas-style environmental-cue effects on surrounding-area violence.

**Proxy outcome (2-5 yr).** Foot-traffic, event volume, 311 complaint density on adjacent blocks, self-reported social cohesion (IMAN community survey).

**Identification strategy.** Case study + ITS on café opening; DiD vs. comparable corners without equivalent investment. Narrative framing rather than causal claim.

**Minimum detectable effect.** Small block-scale; likely not independently detectable outside bundle.

**Threats to validity.** Bundle entanglement; no comparison node.

**Show the investor:** narrative + descriptive engagement metrics + cross-reference to RC2 framework.
**Show the client:** café-operations metrics + community-programming calendar.

## Summary for A8

Six IM initiatives. IM1, IM2, IM3, IM4 form the analytic core (all have clear causal pathways and collectable data). IM5 and IM6 are evaluated descriptively with borrowed effect sizes where applicable. The dominant analytic pattern across the IM domain is *bundle effect* — IMAN's explicit thesis that co-location multiplies outcomes. Evaluation must prioritize bundle metrics (A0 §6) over single-program isolation.
