# Cedar-Riverside ("Little Mogadishu") — Minneapolis, MN

**Headline:** The largest Somali-American enclave in the US. The data here is **mixed and partial** — Cedar-Riverside does NOT have a clean published positive-deviance LE gap. We keep this profile because Cedar-Riverside is the closest structural analog to IMAN's Muslim-immigrant-and-refugee base, and because it offers a cautionary tale about what happens when an enclave has density but lacks middle-life healthcare infrastructure.

## Geography and population

- Cedar-Riverside neighborhood, Minneapolis. Riverside Plaza ("the Crack Stacks" / "Little Mogadishu").
- Also called "Seven Corners" / Minneapolis Ward 6.
- Population ~8,300 in the neighborhood proper; wider Somali-Minneapolis population ~50,000+.

## Demographics

- Estimated 40–60% Somali (in the Riverside Plaza subsection, higher); Minneapolis-wide Somali population is the largest in the US.
- Very high foreign-born share among Somali residents.
- Low median income; very high poverty.
- 34% of Somalis in Minnesota ages 25–64 do not have high school diploma (vs 6% Minnesotans).

## Life expectancy — the honest finding

- **Cedar-Riverside-specific LE is not cleanly published**; USALEEP tract SEs for this small high-Somali tract are large. The headline "Twin Cities low-income neighborhood LE" figure is ~74 years; high-income areas ~82 (MDH data cited in HealthPartners report).
- Somali-specific mortality studies (UCare Circles of Health 2020; HealthPartners Somali study) report **high elderly Somali mortality** — "a daily occurrence" among elders per community informants.
- Somali-American cardiovascular risk profile is **elevated**, not reduced (diabetes prevalence high; low physical activity; rising overweight; high-blood-pressure and -cholesterol increases).

## So why is this still in the portfolio?

Because it answers a different question: **when does the immigrant-enclave advantage fail?** Cedar-Riverside has enclave density, faith-community infrastructure (Dar Al-Hijrah + multiple mosques), and tight kin networks — yet it does not post a Hispanic-paradox-style LE gain. Why not?

1. **Refugee, not immigrant.** Most Somali arrivals are refugees, often post-trauma, without the healthy-migrant selection that applies to economic Mexican migrants. The baseline health entering the US is worse.
2. **Interrupted primary care.** Somalia has had no functional national health system since 1991. Arrivals did not receive decades of Cuban-system-style preventive care before arriving. Contrast with Hialeah Cubans.
3. **Dietary transition at arrival.** Unlike rural Mexican diet (which transfers relatively intact), Somali pastoralist diet (camel milk, meat, anjero) does not survive the Minneapolis transition well.
4. **Discrimination as a direct health stressor.** Anti-Muslim and anti-Somali discrimination documented in Minnesota DPH data is a chronic-stress mechanism that attenuates any enclave-protective effect.
5. **Structural racism and healthcare access.** Documented in the Circles of Health report.

## What IMAN could learn

1. **Muslim-immigrant enclave density is necessary but not sufficient for longevity.** IMAN cannot assume that building a Muslim-anchored community center produces the Hispanic-paradox-style outcomes automatically. The Cuban case works; the Somali case is mixed. The difference is primary-care access.
2. **Refugee origin demands trauma-informed primary care from day 1.** IMAN's Health Center should explicitly operate on refugee-adapted protocols for any Somali, Rohingya, Syrian, or Palestinian arrivals it serves.
3. **Dietary continuity matters — but not all immigrant diets transfer equally well.** IMAN's Fresh Market and Oasis Café programming for Somali, Palestinian, and Black American customers should not assume the Mexican-in-Little-Village pattern generalizes; it must be culturally-tested per population.
4. **Primary-care navigation inside the masjid is the highest-leverage intervention.** HealthPartners' Somali community cardiovascular program found significant reductions in BP and HbA1c when screening was delivered *at* the mosque, not at a hospital. This is directly transferable to IMAN.
