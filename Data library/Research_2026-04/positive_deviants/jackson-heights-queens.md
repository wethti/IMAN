# Jackson Heights — Queens, NYC (Community District QN03)

**Headline:** Arguably the most ethnically diverse square mile on earth (Colombian, Ecuadorian, Mexican, Bangladeshi, Nepali, Tibetan, Indian, Filipino). Life expectancy **~83 years — roughly 2 years above NYC average — despite poverty and uninsurance rates above Queens median.**

## Geography and population

- Queens Community District 3: Jackson Heights + North Corona.
- Population ~172,000.

## Demographics (NYC DOHMH CHP 2018; ACS)

- ~63% Hispanic/Latino (Colombian, Ecuadorian, Mexican, Dominican, Peruvian).
- ~19% Asian (Bangladeshi, Nepali, Indian, Tibetan, Filipino, Chinese).
- Foreign-born ~58%.
- Poverty 17%.
- Median income ~$60k.
- Dense pre-war walkups; small-business retail on 74th, 82nd, Roosevelt Ave.

## Life expectancy

- **Jackson Heights CD 3: ~83 years** (NYC DOHMH CHP 2018).
- NYC 2018 median 81.2; CD3 was 1.8 years above median.
- Predicted LE given demographics ~78. **Residual ≈ +5 years.**

## Hypotheses

1. **Multi-enclave Hispanic paradox.** Colombian, Ecuadorian, and Mexican sub-populations each independently show paradox signal in Ruiz 2013 meta-analysis.
2. **South-Asian-enclave effect.** The Bangladeshi, Nepali, and Tibetan sub-populations bring low-smoking, high-fiber, vegetable-heavy diets. Kandula et al. (MASALA study) documented lower CVD mortality in South Asian ethnic-density tracts.
3. **Walkability and density.** Jackson Heights has one of the highest Walk Scores in NYC outside Manhattan (7 train + E/F/M/R/N/W all converge at Roosevelt).
4. **Small-business density.** Roosevelt Ave and 74th St are among the highest small-business density / sq mile in NYC. "Walk to daily destinations" condition satisfied.
5. **Religious density.** Catholic, Hindu, Buddhist, Muslim, Sikh institutions in walking distance. The Buettner-Power-9 faith-community condition is satisfied across five different religions simultaneously — an unusual test case of whether the mechanism is "religion specifically" or "any faith-community structure."
6. **Low smoking.** NYC Community Health Survey shows Jackson Heights below NYC average.
7. **Salmon bias.** Some, especially for Tibetan and Nepali sub-populations; less than Little Village because Central-Andean and Himalayan return migration is smaller in absolute numbers.

## What IMAN could replicate

1. **Multi-ethnic main street (Roosevelt Ave) as a model of pluralist density.** IMAN operates in a multi-racial, multi-faith South/Southwest Side context (Black, Arab, Latino, Muslim, Christian). Jackson Heights proves that pluralism plus density produces longevity — you don't need ethnic uniformity. This is a direct rebuttal to anyone who says "the Hispanic paradox is ancestry-specific."
2. **Faith-community pluralism as a health resource.** Partner across masjid + churches + mosques + community centers, not just within IMAN's own institutions.
3. **Small-business-ownership density.** Jackson Heights residents own the businesses they shop at. Corner Store Campaign plus the Regenerator / Griot Plaza commercial incubation plan is the IMAN analog.
