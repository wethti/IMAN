# West Philadelphia / Spring Garden — Philadelphia, PA (mixed profile)

**Headline:** West Philadelphia contains both some of the lowest-LE tracts in the US (Mantua: 66 years) and some of the highest (Spring Garden: 87+ years). It is a warning case, not a positive-deviance headline. We profile it to document that **in majority-Black urban neighborhoods, the Hispanic-paradox-style "outperform on LE despite poverty" pattern mostly does not appear** — which matters because IMAN's core Englewood and Chicago Lawn constituency is majority-Black. The investor story must not oversell what's transferable.

## Geography

- West Philadelphia: Philadelphia City neighborhoods west of the Schuylkill. Includes Mantua, Mill Creek, Powelton Village, University City.
- Spring Garden: Central Philadelphia neighborhood just north of Center City.

## Life expectancy, published (USALEEP 2010–2015; Philadelphia DPH Health of the City 2018)

- **Mantua:** 66 years. One-third of adults without HS diploma. Majority Black.
- **Mill Creek:** ~67 years.
- **Strawberry Mansion:** ~68 years.
- **Spring Garden:** 87+ years. 97% HS completion. Majority non-Hispanic white + Asian.
- **Philadelphia overall:** 76.4 years.

## What this means for the positive-deviance search

- We searched USALEEP for Philadelphia tracts with (a) majority-Black population, (b) poverty > 25%, and (c) LE > 75. **There are almost none.** The closest candidate is Cedar Park (~19104) at LE ~75, poverty ~22%, non-Hispanic-Black share ~45%. It does not clear the +5-year residual bar; it is approximately at predicted.
- **The Hispanic paradox literature does not have a clean Black-American analog at the neighborhood scale.** Williams & Collins 2001 and subsequent work on "Black-white mortality gaps" show the gap closes primarily at higher income, not via ethnic-density effects.
- This is not a reason to give up on the investor story; it is a reason to be specific about what the mechanism is.

## The one Black-American positive-deviance candidate we could verify

**Seattle's Rainier Valley (98118 ZIP, S. Seattle):** majority-minority, 60% non-white, lower-income than Seattle median, LE ~79 (King County DPH community profiles) — roughly 1–2 years above its predicted LE. Mechanisms proposed in the King County profile are (a) heavy walk-score / transit investment, (b) large African diaspora (Eritrean, Ethiopian, Somali) enclave effect, (c) Asian-diaspora enclave contribution. Effect is smaller than Little Village or Washington Heights. We list this as a secondary case, not a headline.

## What IMAN should take away

1. **Do not over-promise that the Hispanic-paradox +5-to-10-year gain transfers to a majority-Black Englewood.** It does not, automatically. The evidence base is weaker.
2. **The mechanism that DOES transfer is the built-environment + social-density + faith-anchor cluster — which Sampson's Chicago neighborhood work shows is itself protective in Black Chicago neighborhoods (South Shore, for example, has higher collective efficacy than Englewood and higher LE).** This is a Sampson-style claim, not a Hispanic-paradox claim.
3. **Frame the investor narrative as: "What these Latino enclaves prove is that built-environment + social-density + faith-anchor produces longevity. That mechanism is not Latino-specific. It is portable to Englewood/Chicago Lawn if we manufacture it. IMAN is that manufacturing project."**
