# Cross-Deviant Synthesis: The Urban Blue Zone Pattern

**Scope:** This memo synthesizes the 12 neighborhood profiles in this folder and maps the recurring features onto IMAN's existing and planned programs. It is the Blue-Zones-analogue document — the translation of Buettner's Power 9 into an urban-poverty grammar.

## The 7 recurring features

From the confirmed positive deviants (Little Village, Washington Heights, Sunset Park, Manhattan Chinatown, Jackson Heights, East Boston/Chelsea, Boyle Heights/East LA, Hialeah) we extracted the following features that appear in 5 or more of the 8:

| # | Feature | Buettner Power-9 analog | Published literature anchor |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Dense walkable retail corridor with fresh food in 10 minutes | "Move naturally" | Sallis et al. *Lancet* 2016 built-environment review |
| 2 | Faith institution functioning simultaneously as health/social-service hub | "Belong" / "Right outlook" | VanderWeele 2017; Buettner Blue Zones |
| 3 | Multigenerational household norm | "Family first" / "Loved ones first" | Chen & Chen 2019; Buettner |
| 4 | Ethnic-density + small-business ownership — residents own what they shop at | "Right tribe" | Eschbach et al. 2004; Walton 2012 |
| 5 | Traditional dietary pattern retained at first-generation immigrant level | "Plant slant" / "Wine @ 5" variant | Ruiz 2013; Santiago-Torres 2014 |
| 6 | High collective efficacy — willingness to intervene on behalf of neighbors | (Not in Buettner; Sampson's addition) | Sampson, Raudenbush & Earls 1997 |
| 7 | Low smoking, especially among immigrant women; low alcohol | "80% rule" adjacency | NYC DOHMH CHS; Ruiz 2013 |

Feature #6 is the one Buettner's original framework missed. Sampson's collective-efficacy construct is the urban-American-specific addition that the 2040-planning work needs to foreground, because it is measurable in Chicago and because IMAN's Organizing arm is the program that manufactures it.

## The 5 mechanisms that are plausibly replicable by an organization

Crossing features #1–7 against the "replicability" test in `00_method.md` §6 (requires: published epidemiology, manufacturable by an organization, maps to IMAN programs), we get five mechanisms that IMAN can legitimately claim to be building:

### 1. Walkable-corridor manufacturing (feature #1 + #4)

Fresh Market, Corner Store Campaign, Oasis Café, Regenerator, Griot Plaza. 63rd Street in Chicago Lawn and 63rd/Kedzie as the analog of Little Village's 26th Street or Sunset Park's 8th Avenue. The Corner Store Campaign in particular directly replicates the "family-owned-retail retains fresh food supply" mechanism that we see in every positive deviant profile.

### 2. Faith-health-social co-location (feature #2)

IMAN Central + Masjid + IMAN Health Clinic operating as a single physical block. Screening, navigation, and social services running *through* the masjid rather than being referred out of it. VanderWeele's review is the published evidence that this mechanism is worth 4–14 years of life expectancy at the individual level.

### 3. Multigenerational housing + Takaful caregiving circles (feature #3 + #7)

Green ReEntry housing pipeline explicitly designed for 3-generation occupancy. Takaful Health Savings + Caring Circles as the structured caregiving substrate. This is where the "elders don't die earlier of chronic disease, they survive longer" mechanism actually lives — and it is what separates Little Village from Englewood.

### 4. Collective-efficacy building through Organizing (feature #6)

IMAN's Organizing arm + the Reclaim Chicago / Green ReEntry alumni networks + Takaful Circles. This is directly parallel to the Catholic-parish + hometown-association infrastructure in Little Village and Washington Heights. Sampson's work provides the effect-size evidence: a 2-SD gain in collective efficacy → 40% reduction in homicide, ~1.5-year LE gain.

### 5. Culturally-specific food-environment design (feature #5)

Oasis Café menu design, Fresh Market produce mix, and Corner Store Campaign product placement are levers. The Boyle Heights / Hialeah / Little Village evidence is that first-generation immigrant diets outperform US-standard diets; the IMAN analog is ensuring that Black-American, Arab, and Muslim dietary preferences are served by the Fresh Market / Oasis / corner-store system rather than by the gas-station and chain-fast-food alternatives.

## The mechanisms we explicitly do NOT claim IMAN replicates

Three features we will not overclaim:

- **Healthy-migrant selection.** IMAN cannot recruit a healthier population. This is a selection effect, not a manufacturable mechanism.
- **Salmon bias / return migration.** Not transferable and not ethical to engineer.
- **50 years of existing enclave density.** Little Village, Hialeah, and Washington Heights have multi-decade head starts. IMAN is building the conditions that *over time* produce the same density. The 5-year LE effect will take 15–20 years to fully materialize in the data, even if all mechanisms fire perfectly.

## What this means for the investor story

The honest claim IMAN can make:

> In every urban Blue Zone we verified — Little Village in Chicago, Washington Heights and Chinatown in Manhattan, Sunset Park in Brooklyn, Jackson Heights in Queens, East Boston, Boyle Heights in LA, Hialeah in Miami — residents live **5 to 10 years longer than their neighborhood's income and poverty predict.** Seven structural features co-occur across those neighborhoods. Five of the seven are things an organization can intentionally build. IMAN's Fresh Market + Griot Plaza + Oasis Café + Regenerator + Health Clinic + Masjid + Corner Store Campaign + Green ReEntry + Takaful stack is a deliberate, sequenced attempt to manufacture five of the seven in a disinvested majority-Black Chicago neighborhood.

The honest caveat:

> The positive-deviance literature is overwhelmingly based on Latino and Asian immigrant enclaves. The mechanism (density + faith + social capital + family-based food) is not specific to any ancestry — it is specific to institutional infrastructure. But the evidence that the mechanism transfers fully to a majority-Black urban context is thinner than the Hispanic-paradox evidence. Sampson's Chicago-specific collective-efficacy work is the bridge between the two literatures, and it supports the transferability claim — but conservatively, IMAN should expect a 3–5 year LE gain at full build-out, not the 7–10 year gain seen in first-generation immigrant enclaves.

## Measurement commitment

Because LE moves slowly and is hard to attribute, IMAN will track the following leading indicators over the 2026–2040 window (see Stream A initiative-evaluation framework):

- Walk-audit density of 63rd Street retail corridor.
- Sampson-style collective-efficacy survey (replicating PHDCN instrument) at baseline and every 3 years.
- Fresh Market + Corner Store fresh-produce sales as a proxy for dietary transition.
- Masjid-anchored BP + HbA1c screening volumes (HealthPartners Somali-community model).
- 3-generation household share in IMAN ReEntry housing.
- All-cause mortality and USALEEP-equivalent tract-level LE from CDPH as it republishes.
