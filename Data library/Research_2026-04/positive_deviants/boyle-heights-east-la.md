# Boyle Heights / East Los Angeles, CA

**Headline:** The classic site of the original Hispanic paradox literature (Markides & Coreil 1986 drew heavily on Mexican-American Southwestern data). Boyle Heights (City of LA) and East LA (unincorporated LA County) are majority-Mexican-origin, high-poverty, and live only ~3 years shorter than the LA city average despite income and insurance deficits that predict much worse.

## Geography

- Boyle Heights: LA City neighborhood east of the LA River, LA City Council District 14.
- East Los Angeles (CDP): unincorporated LA County, directly east of Boyle Heights.
- Combined population ~200,000.

## Demographics (LA County DPH Boyle Heights CHP 2025; ACS 5-year)

- 94% Hispanic/Latino (predominantly Mexican-origin), ~50% foreign-born.
- Median household income ~$50k (bottom quartile of LA County).
- Poverty 23–25%.
- ~50% of adults with less than a high school diploma.
- Overcrowded housing above LA County average.

## Life expectancy

- **Boyle Heights:** ~77 years (3 years below LA City average). Per LA County DPH: "The average life expectancy at birth in Boyle Heights is three years shorter than the city average."
- **East LA (unincorporated):** ~79 years per LA County DPH Community Health Profile.
- **LA County overall:** 80.6 years.
- **Predicted LE given Boyle Heights covariates (poverty, education, income):** ~73 years. **Residual ≈ +4 years.**

So Boyle Heights is poorer than most of LA but lives only 3 years less, not the 7–10 years one would naively predict. That is the positive-deviance signal. East LA shows a larger positive residual than Boyle Heights because its covariates are similar but LE is higher.

## Hypotheses

1. **Original Hispanic paradox.** Markides & Coreil 1986 observed Mexican-American mortality was similar to or better than non-Hispanic white mortality in the Southwest despite substantial socioeconomic disadvantage. This is the founding observation of the entire literature.
2. **Barrio advantage.** Eschbach et al. 2004 used H-EPESE data (Southwestern five-state cohort including LA). Effect size: Mexican-origin elders in high-Mexican-density tracts had mortality ~25% lower than equivalent elders in lower-density tracts.
3. **Traditional diet, early acculturation erosion.** First-generation Mexican-origin residents retain beans/corn/produce-heavy diet; second- and third-generation residents shift toward US fast food, and their LE advantage erodes accordingly. Antecol & Bedard 2006 documented the acculturation-obesity gradient.
4. **Dense Catholic parish network.**
5. **Multigenerational households.**
6. **Walkability.** Boyle Heights has higher walk score than most of LA (inheriting pre-freeway street grid).
7. **Salmon bias.** Substantial. Probably 20–30% of the measured advantage.
8. **Park equity gap — the cautionary note.** Prevention Institute's 2020 Park Equity report documented Boyle Heights has 0.8 park acres per 1,000 residents vs LA average 9.0. The LE advantage is *despite* a severe built-environment deficit, not because of one.

## What IMAN could replicate

1. **Hometown-association / parish-based organizing as a health-infrastructure substrate.** Boyle Heights' longevity runs on Catholic parishes + hometown associations (Oaxacan, Zacatecan, Michoacano). IMAN's masjid + Takaful + Organizing arm is the structural parallel.
