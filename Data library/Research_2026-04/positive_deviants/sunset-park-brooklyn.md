# Sunset Park — Brooklyn, NYC (Community District BK07)

**Headline:** A poor, majority-immigrant Brooklyn neighborhood (Chinese + Latino) with a life expectancy of **82.6 years, 1.4 years above NYC and ~5 years above what its poverty and low insurance rates predict.**

## Geography and population

- Brooklyn Community District 7: Sunset Park + Windsor Terrace.
- Population 132,721 (NYC DOHMH CHP 2018).
- Tract coverage: roughly Brooklyn tracts 018–118 in the 11220 and 11232 ZIPs.

## Demographics (NYC DOHMH CHP 2018; NYU Langone Sunset Park Data at a Glance 2022; ACS)

- Roughly 40% Hispanic/Latino (Mexican, Dominican, Puerto Rican) + ~30% Asian (predominantly Chinese, including Fuzhounese) + ~25% non-Hispanic white.
- Foreign-born ~45%.
- Median household income ~$55k (bottom third of NYC).
- Poverty 29% (vs. Brooklyn 21%, NYC 20%).
- Rent burden 57% (vs. 52% NYC).
- Higher share uninsured than NYC average.

## Life expectancy

- **Sunset Park CD 7: 82.6 years** (NYC DOHMH CHP 2018).
- NYC citywide 81.2 years (2018), 82.6 years (2023).
- Predicted LE given poverty 29%, uninsured > NYC, median income bottom-third: ~77–78 years. **Residual ≈ +4.5 to +5 years.**

## Hypotheses for the gap

1. **Double immigrant enclave effect.** Sunset Park's Chinese community (8th Avenue, "Brooklyn's Chinatown") and Mexican-origin community (5th Avenue) both show enclave protective effects documented in the barrio-advantage / Asian-enclave literature (Eschbach et al. 2004 for Latinos; Walton 2012 for Chinese enclaves in NYC linking ethnic density to lower mortality).
2. **Lower smoking among immigrant women, lower alcohol across the board.** NYC CHS consistently shows Sunset Park below NYC average on smoking and binge drinking.
3. **Traditional diets.** Both populations bring dietary patterns (Chinese rice + vegetables + fish + tea; Mexican beans + corn + produce) that outperform the American standard diet in cardiovascular cohort studies.
4. **Walkability.** Brooklyn CB7 is 7th among NYC community districts on Walk Score; N/R/D subway and B35/B63 bus give residents daily walking routines.
5. **Family-based food economy.** Dense family-run grocery stores and restaurants on 5th and 8th Avenues; fresh food within 10 minutes is the norm.
6. **Multigenerational households.** Above NYC median.
7. **Salmon bias for Mexican sub-population; return migration to China for Fuzhounese elderly.** Both biases inflate measured LE; the Sunset Park advantage almost certainly has a non-trivial artifact component and we discount ~20–30%.

## What IMAN could replicate

1. **Double-enclave retail streets as health infrastructure.** The 8th Avenue / 5th Avenue model — two parallel "main streets" each anchored in a different immigrant food culture — maps onto IMAN's 63rd Street corridor vision where Fresh Market + Oasis Café + Griot Plaza are the "main street" layer.
2. **Family-owned corner stores as the fresh-food distribution system.** Sunset Park has no Whole Foods. It has 300+ family stores. IMAN's Corner Store Campaign is the exact parallel strategy.
3. **Walkable clinic co-location.** NYU Langone's Sunset Park clinic anchors the street grid; IMAN Health Center at 63rd/Kedzie is the structural analog.
