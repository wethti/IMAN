# Starr County, TX (rural reference, not urban)

**Headline:** The textbook Hispanic paradox site. Rural Texas border county, 97% Hispanic, one of the poorest counties in the US, yet residents "often live to 80 and beyond" (Texas Tribune 2011). Included here as a **reference case** — the mechanisms are partly rural and partly salmon-biased, so this is **not** a direct template for IMAN. But the investor narrative benefits from naming the Hispanic-paradox literature's most famous site.

## Geography and population

- Starr County, Texas. Rio Grande Valley. County seat Rio Grande City. ~65,000 residents.
- ~55 colonias (unincorporated subdivisions lacking piped water, sewer, paved roads).

## Demographics (Census QuickFacts Starr County TX)

- ~97% Hispanic/Latino (predominantly Mexican-origin).
- Median household income ~$30–35k (bottom 1% of US counties).
- Poverty ~28%.
- ~50% of adults with less than high school.
- Uninsured rate among the highest in the US (>25%).

## Life expectancy

- Starr County life expectancy reported in Texas DSHS county data and surfaced in Texas Tribune 2011 reporting: residents routinely live to **80+**.
- Statewide TX LE ~77 pre-COVID.
- **Predicted LE given covariates (extreme poverty, uninsured, <HS):** ~69–71. Observed ~80. **Residual ≈ +9 to +11 years** — the largest in this portfolio.
- This residual is heavily salmon-biased. Palloni & Arias 2004 essentially explain the foreign-born Mexican portion via return migration. Starr County's residual is probably 40–60% artifact.

## Hypotheses

1. **Salmon bias, large.** Proximity to Mexico means end-of-life return migration is cheap and common. A meaningful fraction of deaths that "should" appear in Starr County vital statistics appear in Tamaulipas or Nuevo León instead.
2. **Healthy-migrant selection.** Multi-generational.
3. **Dietary retention.** Rural Mexican food patterns retained into 3rd generation.
4. **Family density.** Household size larger than urban Hispanic norms.
5. **Faith community.** Catholic parish + Protestant evangelical co-dense.

## Why this is NOT a template for IMAN

- Rural, not urban. Population density and built-environment mechanism do not transfer.
- Mechanisms are dominated by artifact (salmon bias) and cultural retention, not by manufactured institutional infrastructure.
- Useful for the investor narrative only as a reference to the paradox literature, not as a design blueprint.
