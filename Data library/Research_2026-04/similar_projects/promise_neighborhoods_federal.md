# Promise Neighborhoods (U.S. Dept. of Education federal program)

## Location & demographics at start
Federal grant program modeled on Harlem Children's Zone; ~75 planning grants and ~20 implementation grants awarded since 2010. Implementation sites include Chula Vista CA, Hayward Promise, Berea KY (Appalachian), Indianola MS (Mississippi Delta), Northside Achievement Zone (Minneapolis), Buffalo NY, Los Angeles Promise, Chicago-Irving Park / Logan Square, Eastern Kentucky. Target neighborhoods uniformly have majority-minority populations, poverty rates >25%, and low educational attainment. Englewood-analogous on most sites.

## Years active, budget, primary funders
2010 present. Federal funding ~$400M cumulative (2010-2024) — comparatively modest; 5-year implementation grants typically $25-30M per site. Required 100% match in non-federal funds. Authorized by Every Student Succeeds Act 2015.

## Intervention components
"Cradle-to-career pipeline" patterned on HCZ: early-childhood programming, K-12 school improvement, college/career access, family and community supports, data and measurement backbone. Each site designs its own mix against a shared federal indicator framework.

## Evaluation design strength
**Weak across the program but improving.** The long-awaited national impact evaluation from IES/Mathematica was scheduled for 2025; quasi-experimental (comparison schools). Individual sites have self-reported performance data against common indicators (school readiness, proficiency, graduation, postsecondary enrollment). A handful have independent quasi-experimental studies: Northside Achievement Zone Minneapolis (MDRC), Berea (Appalachian Research), LA Promise. **No RCT.**

## Measured outcomes
- Northside Achievement Zone Minneapolis: statistically significant improvements in kindergarten readiness (2012-2018), more modest in later grades (MDRC reports).
- Berea Promise Neighborhoods (Appalachian): high-school graduation rates rose from 66% to 92% (Berea self-report); independently unverified.
- Most sites report improved ratings on individual indicators but few have causal estimates against counterfactuals.
- HCZ-founding Promise Neighborhood itself (in NYC area, not HCZ) showed mixed results.
- **Overall synthesis:** the Promise Neighborhoods replication model has not reliably reproduced HCZ-scale academic effects; most sites underperformed against originally projected outcomes.

## What worked / what failed
**Worked:** Federal backbone funding enabled many neighborhoods to build data systems and backbone organizations they could not otherwise afford. Some sites (Northside, Berea) achieved real sub-outcome gains in early childhood and graduation.
**Failed:** Scale of dollars ($25-30M over 5 years) is an order of magnitude below HCZ's annual spend. Without patient capital at HCZ scale, the model does not produce HCZ-scale effects. Several implementation grants were not renewed. Political headwinds (program nearly eliminated in 2017-2020 federal budgets) undermined continuity.

## Transferability to Englewood
Best informs: (1) Governance & measurement — IMAN can adopt Promise Neighborhoods' federal Common Outcomes framework for its 2040 plan dashboard for free. (2) Scale calibration — a Promise Neighborhood–style 5-year, $25-30M investment alone will not close the LE gap in Englewood; IMAN's $40M+ corridor investment and multi-decade horizon are the correct scale. (3) Data backbone — Promise Neighborhoods invested heavily in longitudinal data infrastructure IMAN currently lacks.
