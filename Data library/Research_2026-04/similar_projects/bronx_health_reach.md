# Bronx Health REACH (CDC Racial and Ethnic Approaches to Community Health)

## Location & demographics at start
South Bronx and Central Bronx, New York City. ~1.4M residents across the Bronx; target communities ~55% Hispanic/Latino, ~40% Black; poverty rate ~28-40% across target tracts; highest diabetes and cardiovascular mortality rates in NYC. Bronx has consistently ranked worst-in-state on County Health Rankings. Life expectancy in South Bronx ~79 years (better than Englewood due to Hispanic paradox) but with very high diabetes mortality. Englewood-analogous on poverty, race, disinvestment; different on LE baseline.

## Years active, budget, primary funders
1999 present (25+ years). Founded under CDC's REACH 2010 program; currently funded through CDC REACH, NIH, NYS DOH. Convened by Institute for Family Health. CDC REACH program funding >$34M/year nationally across grantees. Bronx Health REACH designated CDC Center of Excellence in the Elimination of Disparities.

## Intervention components
Coalition of 70+ faith-based, community-based, and healthcare partners. Core components: (1) **Faith-based outreach** — health education, blood-pressure screening, and lifestyle coaching delivered through Black and Latino churches and mosques; (2) **Cardiovascular disparities focus** — hypertension control, diabetes prevention, healthy food access (farmer's markets on hospital campuses); (3) **Policy advocacy** — removing sugary drinks from schools, soda-tax campaigns; (4) **Provider training** on implicit bias and communication with minority patients.

## Evaluation design strength
Mixed. CDC REACH 2010 national evaluation (NYU Center for Health and Public Service Research) used pre/post and comparison-community design. **One randomized faith-based BP intervention published in peer-reviewed literature:** DeVoe et al. (2019 AJPM-equivalent / CDC Preventing Chronic Disease) on Bronx Health REACH's faith-based BP intervention in immigrant communities. Multiple CDC MMWR reports.

## Measured outcomes
- Racial/ethnic disparities in BP control: measurable narrowing in target communities 2003-2015 per CDC MMWR (specific effect sizes vary across MMWR reports).
- Faith-based BP intervention: statistically significant reductions in systolic BP at 6-month follow-up among adult participants in immigrant-serving churches (DeVoe et al. 2019, CDC PCD). Effect size ~4-7 mmHg systolic reduction.
- Soda-tax and school-beverage policy advocacy: contributed to NYC-wide removal of sugary drinks from schools.
- Overall South Bronx diabetes mortality declined 2000-2020 though attribution to REACH specifically is not established.

## What worked / what failed
**Worked:** Faith-based messenger + clinical screening model has real, peer-reviewed, blood-pressure reduction effects in Black and Latino immigrant communities. 25-year durable coalition. Policy advocacy arm produced lasting NYC school-food wins.
**Failed:** Hard to attribute population-level mortality change to REACH specifically given confounding secular trends. Coalition model struggles with measurement discipline outside the RCT-grade BP studies.

## Transferability to Englewood
**Very high relevance — IMAN's structural DNA matches Bronx Health REACH closely.** Best informs: (1) IMAN's faith-based organizing identity combined with FQHC clinical operations is the Bronx Health REACH formula — IMAN should explicitly cite the DeVoe et al. BP RCT in grant applications and LE-impact modeling. (2) CHW + faith-leader messengers is already IMAN's approach — the peer-reviewed evidence supports the model. (3) CDC REACH funding stream is available to IMAN and should be pursued. (4) Policy advocacy arm (SNAP coalition, Green Line) maps onto REACH's advocacy playbook.
