# Pathways Community HUB — Akron / Summit County, OH

## Location & demographics at start
Summit County, Ohio (Akron metro) — target: at-risk pregnant Black women and mothers, and broader populations with complex social needs. Akron 2015 baseline: Black infant mortality rate 14 per 1,000 live births (vs. national ~11 for Black infants, vs. ~5 for white infants statewide). ~28% of Akron Black population below poverty; majority of at-risk target population in historically Black west Akron. Englewood-analogous on maternal-child health disparities, poverty concentration, structural racism.

## Years active, budget, primary funders
Pathways Community HUB model developed 2000s in Mansfield, OH, by Mark and Sarah Redding (MD and RN). Akron Summit Community Action Pathways HUB launched 2013. Primary funders: Ohio Medicaid (MCO contracts), Summit County Public Health, Akron Children's Hospital, Knight Foundation, Sisters of Charity Foundation. Certified HUB model now operates in 20+ U.S. locations; AHRQ/CMS have recognized it.

## Intervention components
Model coordinates Community Health Workers across a regional "HUB" that contracts with multiple CBOs. Features: (1) 21 standardized "Pathways" (smoking cessation, healthy eating, housing, behavioral health, etc.) that a CHW tracks from identification to completion; (2) outcome-based payment — only completed Pathways are reimbursed; (3) centralized registry to prevent duplicate services across agencies; (4) accredited CHW training.

## Evaluation design strength
**Moderate to strong.** AHRQ and CMS reviewed. Cochran-adjusted pre/post evaluation of Mansfield HUB: statistically significant reductions in low-birthweight deliveries among high-risk Black mothers. Multiple studies published in Maternal and Child Health Journal, American Journal of Public Health. Not RCT — policy-system intervention hard to randomize — but the cleanest community-CHW evidence in the U.S.

## Measured outcomes
- **Mansfield Community HUB pilot: low-birthweight deliveries reduced to ~7% in enrolled high-risk Black mothers vs. ~13% in a matched comparison population** (Redding et al., Maternal and Child Health Journal 2015).
- Akron Pathways HUB: 2014-2020 served thousands of at-risk mothers and families; self-reported process outcomes strong.
- Akron Black infant mortality rate fell from 14 per 1,000 in 2015 toward ~11 per 1,000 by 2021 (Summit County Public Health data), although attribution to HUB alone cannot be established.
- Cost-effectiveness: Milbank Memorial Fund estimates HUB model saves $5-7 per $1 invested through reduced NICU and ED costs.
- Commonwealth Fund case study documents regional reductions in potentially avoidable hospitalizations and unplanned readmissions.

## What worked / what failed
**Worked:** Outcome-based CHW payment is a genuine innovation that solves the fidelity/accountability problem exposed by Camden Coalition's null result. The Pathways framework converts CHW work into measurable units. Medicaid MCOs will pay for completed Pathways — a sustainable financing model that does not depend solely on grants.
**Failed / limits:** Model fidelity is demanding — requires accredited CHWs, shared registry, outcome verification. Many "Pathways HUB" replications do not implement with full fidelity. Not yet tested at large urban scale beyond Ohio.

## Transferability to Englewood
**One of the strongest matches for IMAN's CHW team.** Best informs: (1) **IMAN's CDPH-funded CHW team should adopt Pathways HUB's outcome-based payment framework** — this solves the Camden null problem and opens Medicaid MCO revenue. (2) IMAN could partner with Chicago Community Health Equity Coalition and CDPH to certify a Pathways HUB for Englewood/Chicago Lawn. (3) The Mansfield low-birthweight evidence is a direct citation for IMAN's maternal-child health gap (profile identifies this as a missing program area). (4) The 5-7:1 ROI is a defensible grant/Medicaid-contract number.
