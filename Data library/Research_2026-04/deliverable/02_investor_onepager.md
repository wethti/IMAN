# Investor One-Pager — The Urban Blue Zone Next Door
**Audience:** funders, philanthropic capital, mission-aligned investors | **Length:** 1 page

## The headline
**A neighborhood ten miles from Englewood has fourteen more years of life expectancy.**

Englewood (Chicago Community Area 68), 2023: **life expectancy 67.7 years.** (Chicago Department of Public Health, 2023 Life Expectancy Data Brief.) Little Village / La Villita (Community Area 30), 2023: **~82 years.** Same city, same CDPH data pipeline, same poverty profile, same food-desert designation, same pre-1940 housing stock.

That is a 14-year gap inside one city, between two equally-poor neighborhoods. After Palloni & Arias's salmon-bias / healthy-migrant adjustment, Little Village's LE advantage is still 9–11 years. The gap is not a data artifact.

## What explains the gap — four replicable features

Across every confirmed urban positive deviant — Little Village (Chicago), Washington Heights (NYC, 84.0 yrs), Sunset Park (Brooklyn, 82.6 yrs), Manhattan Chinatown (82.2 yrs), Jackson Heights (Queens, ~83 yrs), East Boston/Chelsea (~80 yrs), Boyle Heights (LA, ~78 yrs), Hialeah (Miami, ~81 yrs) — four structural features co-occur (full synthesis: [positive_deviants/02_synthesis.md](../positive_deviants/02_synthesis.md)):

1. **Walk to daily destinations in your cultural idiom.** 26th Street in Little Village; 8th Avenue in Sunset Park; Roosevelt Avenue in Jackson Heights. Residents walk to fresh food, clinic, worship, and social ties. Englewood's 63rd Street corridor was built for this and has been emptied out by 50 years of disinvestment.
2. **A faith institution that is also a health and social-services institution.** Our Lady of Tepeyac (Little Village); Charles B. Wang Community Health Center (Chinatown); Dar Al-Hijrah (Somali Minneapolis). VanderWeele (Harvard, JAMA 2017) finds weekly religious-service attendance associated with 4 to 14 additional years of life expectancy, net of baseline health.
3. **Multigenerational households with intentional elder caregiving.** 3-generation co-residence is substantially above Chicago average in Little Village. Elders do not avoid chronic disease; they *survive longer with it* because household caregiving exists.
4. **Small-business-owner economy, not a chain economy.** Residents own what they shop at. Corner stores sell fresh produce. The "mercado economy" resists ultra-processed food substitution.

Add Sampson, Raudenbush & Earls (*Science* 1997) — a Chicago-specific addition measuring **collective efficacy**. 2-SD gain in collective efficacy → 40% lower homicide rate and substantial reductions in all-cause mortality. Little Village's collective-efficacy score substantially exceeds Englewood's.

## Why this is an investment, not a donation

Five project benchmarks from [similar_projects/](../similar_projects/) that combined healthcare + food + housing + workforce in a single geographic corridor and produced measured outcomes:

- **East Lake, Atlanta (Purpose Built Communities).** 1995–present. Moved from 96th-percentile US poverty to demographic parity with metro Atlanta over 20 years. Employment among residents rose from 13% to 70%+.
- **Dudley Street Neighborhood Initiative, Boston (Roxbury).** 1984–present. 30-year community-land-trust model. The Boston case the client recalled.
- **Harlem Children's Zone.** Zone-wide. School-age Black students closing achievement gap with NYC white students in HCZ Promise Academy charter grades.
- **Philadelphia LandCare.** 2000–present. Full 2007–2023 DiD: −30% aggravated assault, −31% firearm aggravated assault, −15% firearm violent crime (all CIs exclude 0).
- **READI Chicago.** 2017–present. RCT of 18-month CBT + transitional jobs for highest-risk men: primary outcome null, but −65% shooting/homicide arrests in full sample, −79% in outreach-referred subgroup. BCR 4:1 to 18:1.

## What's honest

Neighborhood-level LE moves slowly and is noisy. We cannot guarantee a specific LE change in year three. We *can* guarantee: the proxy indicators above will move on that horizon if the programs work, and the peer-matched synthetic control against the client's 16-community pool will tell us whether Englewood's trajectory diverged from its peers. The positive-deviance literature is overwhelmingly Latino/Asian immigrant; the mechanism should transfer but the evidence is thinner for majority-Black contexts. Conservative expectation: **3–5 years of LE gain at full build-out over 10–15 years**, not the 7–10 seen in first-generation immigrant enclaves.

## The ask

A **$25M catalytic capital commitment** to complete the 63rd Street corridor build-out (Fresh Market expansion + Corner Store saturation + Oasis Café + Griot Plaza + Regenerator). On the published evidence, this is the cheapest 5-year-LE-gain intervention the literature supports that is *implementable today* in a US disinvested neighborhood.
