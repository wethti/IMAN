The thesis

Don't fund a program. Fund the ecosystem.

Twenty years of single-program interventions in Englewood — grocery stores, clinics, violence crews — have each worked on their own terms. Life expectancy has barely moved. The gap is driven by a stack of interacting risks, and only a stacked response, on the same blocks, for long enough, collapses it. IMAN has been assembling that stack for 25 years. The 2040 plan runs it at the density where the evidence says compounding effects appear.

About IMAN

The Inner-City Muslim Action Network is a 25-year-old Chicago institution rooted in Chicago Lawn and Englewood, operating across health, housing, arts, reentry, food access, and community organizing. The 2040 plan integrates these pillars into a single ecosystem of care measured against the 16 U.S. communities that most resemble Englewood.
