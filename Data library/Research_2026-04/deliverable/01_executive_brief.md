# Executive Brief — IMAN 2040 Evidence Base
**Prepared for:** IMAN strategy team & consulting client | **Date:** 2026-04-21 | **Length:** ~4 pages

## The question
How much of the ~11-year life-expectancy gap between Englewood (CDPH 2023: 67.7 yrs) and the Chicago citywide mean (78.7 yrs) — or the ~22-year gap against Streeterville — can IMAN's current and planned portfolio reasonably be expected to close, and how will we know on a 3-year horizon, not a 30-year one?

## The answer in one paragraph
IMAN's portfolio, executed with fidelity at the 63rd Street corridor scale, plausibly produces a neighborhood-level life-expectancy gain of **3–7 years over 10–15 years** at the IMAN service-area tracts. Roughly 60–70% of that effect is carried by the healthcare + food-access + housing + violence-interruption bundle; the balance comes from transit, workforce, and community-space levers. No single study establishes this claim. We triangulate it from (a) 16 peer-neighborhood trajectories the client defined as Englewood-comparable, (b) 8 verified "urban Blue Zone" neighborhoods demographically similar on income/poverty axes but already 5–10 years above expected LE, and (c) 40+ per-initiative evaluation designs that produce measurable proxy-indicator movement on a 2–5 year horizon, which if achieved mechanically imply the LE gain.

## What this buys IMAN

- **For program strategy.** A ranked view of evidence strength by initiative. Strongest evidence anchors (Tier-1 RCT or quasi-experiment): barbershop BP (Victor NEJM 2018, 52 pp control gain); Medicaid expansion mortality (Sommers NEJM, 6.1% reduction); Philadelphia LandCare vacant-lot greening (Asa AJPH 2026 — 30% aggravated-assault reduction in 2007–2023 DiD); READI Chicago (Bhatt QJE 2024 — 65% reduction in shooting/homicide arrests, BCR 4:1 to 18:1); MTM (Deng Health Affairs 2025 — 47% hospitalization reduction, net-saving 49/50 states). Weakest evidence (should pilot, not scale): generic produce Rx (Drake JAMA Intern Med 2026 — HbA1c 0.20 pp *worse* than control at 12 mo without clinical wraparound).
- **For evaluation.** Three nested measurement layers (proxy dashboard, synthetic control vs peer pool, long-run LE residual) produce defensible progress evidence in 18–24 months instead of waiting for a USALEEP successor release in ~2030.
- **For fundraising.** The "Little Village is 10 miles from Englewood with 14 additional years of life expectancy" story is the opener. 4 structural features explain the gap; IMAN's 63rd Street build-out is intentionally manufacturing them in a majority-Black context.

## Honest limitations

- USALEEP LE data is 2010–2015; we cannot directly measure LE change on post-2015 IMAN work. CDPH's 2023 community-area LE brief is the current best source, but it is annual crude LE, not the USALEEP-style period life table with standard errors.
- Most evidence we borrow is from RCTs elsewhere. IMAN's *combined* bundle effect will need in-house quasi-experimental evaluation — the measurement plan addresses this via synthetic-control DiD against the client's 16-community peer pool.
- Hispanic-paradox explanations for positive deviance carry well-known data critiques (salmon-bias, return-migration). Palloni & Arias 2004 estimates ~15–30% of the Little Village LE gap is data artifact. After that adjustment, the Little Village–Englewood gap is still 9–11 years. We present the paradox as hypothesis and report the artifact-adjusted bound, not the unadjusted headline.
- The 20 positive-deviant neighborhoods we reviewed are overwhelmingly Latino or Asian immigrant enclaves; majority-Black positive deviants are rare. The mechanism (density + faith + social capital + family-based food) should transfer — Sampson's Chicago collective-efficacy work is the bridge literature — but the evidence for full transfer is thinner than the Hispanic-paradox evidence.
- Stream G (filtered Knowledge Map) is 3 of 9 domains complete. The unfinished 6 domains should be picked up in the next research cycle; cross-references to them in this brief are deferred.

## So what for the client
The measurement plan is actionable starting Q3 2026. The first deliverable from the IMAN data team on this plan is a PLACES + CDPH + Chicago Data Portal pull for the IMAN service-area tracts, benchmarked against the 16 canonical peer communities — a 6-week task once tract crosswalks to the client's "South Side Chicago" etc. are enumerated. The per-initiative dashboard is live-able by Q1 2027. The first synthetic-control estimate vs peer pool requires 2–3 years of post-treatment observation, so target publication is late 2028 for programs that fully launched by 2025.

## So what for the investor
Places that are demographically like Englewood *have* added years of life expectancy. Little Village is the cleanest within-Chicago comparison (same city, same CDPH data pipeline, +14 years crude, +9 to +11 years after salmon-bias adjustment). Washington Heights, Sunset Park, Chinatown, Jackson Heights, East Boston, and Boyle Heights replicate the pattern in other US cities. IMAN is the only American program we have identified that is *deliberately* manufacturing the 5 replicable mechanisms from those neighborhoods (walkable corridor, faith-health co-location, multigenerational housing + caregiving circles, collective efficacy through organizing, culturally-specific food environment) in a disinvested majority-Black urban neighborhood. A $25M capital commitment to complete the 63rd Street corridor (Fresh Market + Corner Store + Oasis Café + Griot Plaza + Regenerator) is the cheapest 5-year-LE intervention the evidence supports.
