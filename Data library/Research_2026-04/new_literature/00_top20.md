# Top 20 Papers Added to the Knowledge Map (2023-2026)

Ranked by relevance × evidence tier. Compiled by Stream F, 2026-04-21.

| Rank | Paper | Why it matters |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | **Graetz et al., Soc Sci Med 2024** — rent burden & eviction mortality | Eviction judgment = 40% higher mortality (36-43% CI); rent burden +20 pts = 16% higher mortality. Linked Census → Numident → 38M eviction records. Directly quantifies IMAN's eviction-prevention life-years saved. |
| 2 | **Bergman/Chetty et al., AER 2024** — Creating Moves to Opportunity | Customized housing-search assistance raised share moving to high-opportunity tracts 15%→53%. First causal identification of the barrier that matters. Changes IMAN's housing advocacy ask. |
| 3 | **Deng/Mozaffarian et al., Health Affairs 2025** — MTM in 50 states | 47% fewer hospitalizations (95% CI 36-58%), 19.7% cost savings, net-saving in 49/50 states. Most credible national ROI for food-is-medicine. Direct business-case for Medicaid-funded Fresh Market food-is-medicine line. |
| 4 | **Bhatt/Heller et al., QJE 2024** — READI Chicago | RCT of 18-mo CBT+job for highest-risk men; primary outcome null but -65% shooting/homicide arrests and -79% in outreach-referred subgroup. BCR 4:1-18:1. Anchors IMAN's violence-intervention arm. |
| 5 | **Hager et al., Health Affairs 2025** — MA Medicaid Nutrition Supports | -23% hospitalizations, -13% ED visits, -$1,721 per member post-COVID. Evaluated the actual 1115 mechanism IMAN wants IL to copy. |
| 6 | **Vivalt/Rhodes/Bartik et al., NBER 2024** — UBI Employment | Largest US UBI RCT; -4.1 pp labor-force participation, -1-2 hrs/wk worked. Honest evidence IMAN must engage when arguing for cash transfers. |
| 7 | **Asa et al., AJPH 2026** — Vacant-Lot DiD 2007-2023 | 30% reduction in aggravated assault, 31% in firearm aggravated assault, 15% in firearm violent crime (all 95% CI excludes zero). Extends Branas 2018 evidence through 2023. |
| 8 | **Doyle/Alsan et al., JAMA Intern Med 2024** — Food-as-Medicine null | Null on HbA1c (adjusted diff -0.10; 95% CI -0.46 to 0.25). Counterweight IMAN must cite honestly. |
| 9 | **Drake et al., JAMA Intern Med 2026** — Produce Rx RCT | HbA1c 0.20 pp higher with subsidy; only 30% used ≥80% of benefit. IMAN should design produce-Rx with clinical wraparound or skip. |
| 10 | **Bartik/Vivalt et al., NBER 2024** — UBI Consumption | +$300/mo household spending; MPC on non-durables 0.44-0.55. Quantifies how cash is actually used. |
| 11 | **Van Vleet, NC Med J 2024** — NC Healthy Opportunities Pilots | 265k services to 20k enrollees, $47M reimbursed, $85/month/enrollee savings. Direct blueprint for IL. |
| 12 | **Van Iseghem et al., Value in Health 2026** — CHW cost-effectiveness systematic review | 70% of CHW interventions cost-effective; median cost/QALY $3,967. Benchmarks CHW pricing. |
| 13 | **Fischer et al., BMC Public Health 2025** — Naloxone meta-analysis | 98.3% survival when administered by PWUD. Definitive scale-up evidence. |
| 14 | **West & Castro, J Urban Health 2023** — Stockton SEED | Kessler-10 distress -2.9 pts (P=.027); income volatility 19% vs 26% (P=.039). Strongest individual-level cash→health RCT result. |
| 15 | **Schillok et al., JAMA Psychiatry 2025** — Collaborative Care depression IPD meta-analysis | 35 datasets, 20k participants; therapeutic-treatment-strategy component drives effect. Informs Fresh Market co-located mental-health design. |
| 16 | **Hamad et al., Health Affairs 2023** — CTC adult mental health | Quasi-experimental — fewer depressive/anxiety symptoms for low-income adults; larger reductions for POC parents. Underpins Fresh Boost + CTC advocacy. |
| 17 | **HUD OPDR, 2024** — Choice Neighborhoods evaluation | Mixed-methods of first 9 grantees; residents saw income/employment gains; neighborhood-level impacts harder to attribute. Realistic expectations for HUD-scale wins. |
| 18 | **Aizer/Lleras-Muney/Michelmore, NBER 2024** — CTC child outcomes | Synthesizes short- and long-run child-health gains concentrated in poor children. |
| 19 | **Liu et al., Meta-analysis 2024** — Green space → psychiatric disorders | OR 0.89-0.91 reductions. Population-level evidence for Griot Plaza. |
| 20 | **Cure Violence systematic review, 2025** | Mixed effects; 37-63% reductions where fidelity high; null elsewhere. Forces IMAN to pair CVI with fidelity-monitoring. |
