# F4 — Education & Youth Development — New Literature 2023-2026

1. **Bhatt MP, Heller SB, Kapustin M, Bertrand M, Blattman C. (2024).** "Predicting and Preventing Gun Violence: An Experimental Evaluation of READI Chicago." *Quarterly Journal of Economics* 139(1):1-56. NBER WP 30852, DOI: 10.3386/w30852. URL: https://www.nber.org/papers/w30852. OA: partial. Design: RCT (N=2,456). Effect: primary violence index — not statistically significant; shooting/homicide arrests -65% (P=.13 adjusted); among outreach-worker-referred subgroup -79% arrests, -43% victimizations (statistically significant after adjustment); social savings $182k-$916k/participant (P=.03); BCR 4:1-18:1. Relevance: flagship CBT+transitional-jobs evidence for highest-risk men; READI is an IMAN peer program. Tier 1.

2. **Davis JMV, Heller SB. (2020) [SUPPLEMENTARY].** "Rethinking the Benefits of Youth Employment Programs: The Heterogeneous Effects of Summer Jobs." *Review of Economics and Statistics* 102(4):664-677. Effect: violent-crime arrests declined during and after summer; machine-learning uncovered heterogeneity. Retained because no superseding 2023-2026 long-term follow-up has yet been published. Tier 1.

3. **Aizer A, Lleras-Muney A, Michelmore K. (2024).** "The Effects of the 2021 Child Tax Credit on Child Developmental Outcomes." NBER WP 32609. DOI: 10.3386/w32609. URL: https://www.nber.org/papers/w32609. OA: Y. Design: synthesis / quasi-experimental. Effect: likely improved child health short- and long-run, with greater effects for poor children; effects concentrated in younger children and weak-safety-net places. Relevance: supports Fresh Boost-style cash + CTC advocacy. Tier 2.

4. **Hamad R, Collin DF, Gemmill A, Jackson K, Wherry L. (2023).** "Effects of the 2021 Expanded Child Tax Credit on Adults' Mental Health: A Quasi-Experimental Study." *Health Affairs* 42(1). DOI: 10.1377/hlthaff.2022.00733. URL: https://www.healthaffairs.org/doi/10.1377/hlthaff.2022.00733. OA: N. Design: quasi-experimental DiD. Effect: fewer depressive/anxiety symptoms among low-income adults; larger reductions among Black, Hispanic, other POC parents than non-Hispanic White. Relevance: CTC as mental-health intervention for Englewood parents. Tier 1.

5. **Castro A, West S, Springboard to Opportunities. (2024).** "The Magnolia Mother's Trust 2023-2024 Evaluation & Case Study Report." Ascend at the Aspen Institute. URL: https://springboardto.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/MMT-2023-2024-Eval-Case-Study-Report.pdf. OA: Y. Design: mixed-methods evaluation (cohort of low-income Black Mississippi mothers; $1,000/mo × 12 mo). Effect: improved financial stability, parenting time, maternal wellbeing (qualitative + quantitative). Relevance: race-/gender-centered guaranteed income directly analogous to Englewood cohort IMAN serves. Tier 3.
