# F9 — Cross-Cutting SDOH — New Literature 2023-2026

1. **Bergman et al. (2024) — Creating Moves to Opportunity** — see F3.1. Most important cross-cutting neighborhood paper of 2024.

2. **Graetz et al. (2024) — rent burden + eviction mortality** — see F3.2. Single most important cross-cutting SDOH-mortality paper of 2023-2026.

3. **Humphries C, et al. (2025).** "A Meta-Analysis of Neighbourhood Interventions on Subjective Wellbeing and Mental Health." *Applied Research in Quality of Life*. DOI: 10.1007/s11482-025-10440-2. URL: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11482-025-10440-2. OA: partial. Design: meta-analysis (54 studies, 29 interventions). Effect: short-term wellbeing/mental-health gains from place-based interventions, with heterogeneous moderators. Relevance: supports IMAN's bundle approach; flags attribution difficulty. Tier 1.

4. **Freedman M, Khanna G, Neumark D. (2025).** "Place-Based Policies for Neighborhood Improvement: Evidence from Promise Zones." arXiv:2503.05946. URL: https://arxiv.org/html/2503.05946v1. OA: Y. Design: quasi-experimental. Effect: Promise Zones reduced neighborhood poverty ~2× Empowerment Zones + NMTC; ~1/3 the effect size of HOPE VI. Relevance: place-based policy cost-effectiveness hierarchy informs IMAN's funding asks. Tier 2.

5. **Hager et al. (2025) — MA Flexible Services** — see F1.2.

6. **Van Vleet (2024) — NC Healthy Opportunities Pilots** — see F1.3.

7. **Chetty R, Friedman JN, Hendren N, Jones MR, Porter SR. (2026).** "The Opportunity Atlas: Mapping the Childhood Roots of Social Mobility." *American Economic Review* 116(1). URL: https://rajchetty.com/publications/. OA: partial. Design: administrative tract-level panel. Effect: updated tract-level opportunity estimates for cohorts born 1978-1983 with an additional decade of follow-up through the 2020s. Relevance: Englewood/Chicago Lawn tract-level comparison baseline. Tier 1.
