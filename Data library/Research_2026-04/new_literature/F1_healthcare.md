# F1 — Healthcare — New Literature 2023-2026

Added to the Knowledge Map by Stream F on 2026-04-21. All citations verified via PubMed/DOI where possible.

1. **Van Iseghem T, Vroonen L, Op de Beeck E, Meertens A, Masquillier C, Wouters E, Verhaeghe N. (2026).** "The Cost-Effectiveness of Community Health Workers in Primary Health Care: A Systematic Review." *Value in Health* 29(2):324-344. DOI: 10.1016/j.jval.2025.09.004. URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40983309/. OA: partial. Design: systematic review (50 studies, 25 countries). Effect: 35/50 (70%) of CHW interventions cost-effective vs usual care; median cost/QALY $3,966.50 (2024 US$); median cost/DALY averted $703. Relevance-to-IMAN: benchmarks the dollar-per-QALY IMAN's CHW staffing should target when pricing the Fresh Market clinic bundle. Tier 1.

2. **Hager K, Sabatino M, Williams J, Ash AS, Halasa-Rappel Y, Flahive JM, Min HS, Sing G, Buckler S, Rich A, Bowman J, Himmelstein J, Alcusky MJ. (2025).** "Medicaid Nutrition Supports Associated With Reductions In Hospitalizations And ED Visits In Massachusetts, 2020-23." *Health Affairs* 44(4):413-421. DOI: 10.1377/hlthaff.2024.01409. URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40193848/. OA: N. Design: quasi-experimental (20,403 participants, 17 ACOs). Effect: 23% fewer hospitalizations, 13% fewer ED visits; costs $1,721 lower per member post-COVID. Relevance: direct precedent for Medicaid-funded SDOH services at scale — a 1115 model IMAN could advocate for IL. Tier 1.

3. **Van Vleet A. (2024).** "Reflecting on Nearly Two Years of North Carolina's Healthy Opportunities Pilots." *North Carolina Medical Journal* 85(2). DOI: 10.18043/001c.94844. URL: https://ncmedicaljournal.com/article/94844. OA: Y. Design: descriptive program evaluation. Effect: 265,000+ services to 20,000+ enrollees, $47M in reimbursements; 95% auth approval; state-reported $85/enrollee/month in medical cost savings. Relevance: first-in-nation Medicaid 1115 paying for food/housing/transportation — blueprint for IL 1115 ask. Tier 2.

4. **Schillok H, Gensichen J, Panagioti M, et al. (POKAL Group). (2025).** "Effective Components of Collaborative Care for Depression in Primary Care: An Individual Participant Data Meta-Analysis." *JAMA Psychiatry* 82(9):868-876. DOI: 10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2025.0183. URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40136273/. OA: N. Design: IPD meta-analysis (35 datasets, 20,046 participants). Effect: therapeutic-treatment-strategy component produced largest effect on depression (interaction coefficient -0.07, P<0.001). Relevance: supports co-location of CBT/family-involvement with primary care at Fresh Market clinic. Tier 1.

5. **Koumpias AM, Chawla D, Schwartz AL. (2024).** "Revisiting the connection between state Medicaid expansions and adult mortality." *Southern Economic Journal* 91(2). URL: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/soej.12719. OA: N. Design: quasi-experimental DiD replication. Effect: using state-level rather than county-level data substantially attenuates Sommers et al. (2012) mortality-reduction point estimate; authors conclude Medicaid-expansion mortality signal is less precisely estimated than originally reported. Relevance: important methodological check — IMAN should quote Medicaid-expansion mortality effects as a range (near-zero to 6%), not a point estimate. Tier 2.

6. **Fischer LS, Asher A, Stein R, Becasen J, Doreson A, Mermin J, Meltzer MI, Edlin BR. (2025).** "Effectiveness of naloxone distribution in community settings to reduce opioid overdose deaths among people who use drugs: a systematic review and meta-analysis." *BMC Public Health* 25(1):1135. DOI: 10.1186/s12889-025-22210-8. URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40133970/. OA: Y. Design: systematic review/meta-analysis. Effect: survival after naloxone administration 98.3% (95% CI 97.5-98.8%) when delivered by people who use drugs; 95.0% (91.4-97.1%) family/community; 92.4% (88.9-94.8%) police. Community-level: 27-46% reduction in opioid OD mortality in low/high-implementing communities. Relevance: updates MA Walley 2013 evidence with 2025 saturation data — directly supports IMAN's naloxone distribution scale-up. Tier 1.
