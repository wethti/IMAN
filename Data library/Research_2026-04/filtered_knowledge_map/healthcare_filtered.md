## Retained studies

### 1. Finkelstein A et al. — The Oregon Health Insurance Experiment: Evidence from the First Year
- **Path:** `Knowledge Map/Healthcare/1 - Finkelstein - The Oregon Health Insurance Experiment Evidence from the First Year.pdf`
- **Why qualifies:** Oregon Medicaid lottery, population = low-income uninsured US adults. Matches criterion 1 (income), 4 (SNAP/EBT-eligible income tier). Population and setting directly analogous to uninsured Englewood residents (Englewood has high uninsured rate).
- **Effect size (verbatim from `evidence_base.md`):** "Lottery winners were significantly more likely to have a usual place of care, receive preventive care, and report better physical and mental health." (Abstract-quality magnitudes not in evidence_base; mark as `[EFFECT MAGNITUDE — retrieve full PDF for exact ORs]`.)
- **Informs IMAN initiative(s):** FQHC expansion, Medicaid enrollment outreach, CHW enrollment program
- **Evidence tier:** 1 (RCT)

### 2. NEJMsa — Mortality and Access to Care Among Adults After State Medicaid Expansions
- **Path:** `Knowledge Map/Healthcare/4 - NEJMsa - Mortality and Access to Care among Adults after State Medicaid Expansions.pdf`
- **Why qualifies:** US state-level quasi-experiment. Population = near-poor US adults in Medicaid-expansion states. Criterion 1 (income). Directly applicable to uninsured Englewood adults.
- **Effect size (verbatim):** "6.1% reduction in all-cause mortality among near-poor adults compared to non-expansion states... A 6% mortality reduction in a population with a 30-year life expectancy gap translates to approximately 0.7–1.5 years of gap closure."
- **Informs:** FQHC expansion (~1.5 yr), Medicaid enrollment outreach (~0.7 yr)
- **Evidence tier:** 1 (quasi-experiment)

### 3. NEJMoa — A Cluster-Randomized Trial of Blood-Pressure Reduction in Black Barbershops
- **Path:** `Knowledge Map/Healthcare/2 - NEJMoa - A Cluster-Randomized Trial of Blood-Pressure Reduction in Black Barbershops.pdf` (duplicate also at entry 3 in folder)
- **Why qualifies:** Population = Black men in LA barbershops, exactly the trusted-community-venue intervention IMAN uses. Matches criterion 5 (demographics: majority-Black, college <30%), criterion 6 (urban retail corridor delivery).
- **Effect size (verbatim):** "systolic BP control in 63.6% of participants vs. 11.7% in controls... reduces stroke risk by ~35–40% and cardiovascular mortality by ~25%."
- **Informs:** Barbershop/beauty salon HTN & diabetes screening (~0.6 yr); community node → health outcome chain (Recreation domain overlap)
- **Evidence tier:** 1 (cluster RCT)

### 4. Macinko J — Brazil Family Health Strategy: Community-Based Primary Care
- **Path:** `Knowledge Map/Healthcare/2 - Macinko James et al - Brazil's Family Health Strategy Delivering Community-Based Primary Care in a Universal Health System.pdf`
- **Why qualifies:** `[TRANSFERABILITY CAVEAT]` Brazil national program, NOT a peer setting. Retained because the CHW-based primary care mechanism is structurally identical to IMAN's FQHC + CHW model and it is the strongest documented effect of that mechanism. Poor-state sub-analyses are the relevant transferable subset.
- **Effect size (verbatim):** "significant reduction in infant mortality and overall mortality, particularly in poorer states."
- **Informs:** FQHC expansion, CHW team deployment
- **Evidence tier:** 2 (national observational with policy variation)

### 5. Mortality Risk During and After Opioid Substitution Treatment (MOUD) — systematic review/meta-analysis
- **Path:** `Knowledge Map/Healthcare/7 - Unknown Author - Mortality risk during and after opioid substitution treatment systematic review and meta-analysis of cohort studies.pdf`
- **Why qualifies:** Systematic review of cohort studies; MOUD effect size is well-replicated in US urban opioid populations. Opioid overdose is a major driver of premature mortality in Englewood.
- **Effect size (verbatim):** "Risk of death during treatment was 50–75% lower than off-treatment periods."
- **Informs:** Naloxone + MOUD expansion (~0.8 yr combined estimate)
- **Evidence tier:** 1 (systematic review/meta-analysis)

### 6. Walley AY et al. — Opioid Overdose Rates and Naloxone Distribution, Massachusetts
- **Path:** `Knowledge Map/Healthcare/1 - Alexander Y Walley et al - Opioid overdose rates and implementation of overdose education and nasal naloxone distribution in Massachusetts interrupted time series analysis.pdf`
- **Why qualifies:** Interrupted time series in US urban communities. Massachusetts community cohorts include low-income, majority-minority urban tracts. Mechanism directly transferable.
- **Effect size (verbatim):** "46% lower opioid overdose mortality than low-distribution communities."
- **Informs:** Naloxone distribution, overdose response program (~0.8 yr)
- **Evidence tier:** 1 (quasi-experiment)
