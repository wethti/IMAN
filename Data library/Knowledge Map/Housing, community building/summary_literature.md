# Housing and Community Building - Literature Summary

The evidence is unambiguous: neighbourhood conditions are an independent cause of premature death, not just a background factor. Housing instability accelerates biological ageing (Geronimus weathering), concentrated poverty raises cardiometabolic mortality even after controlling for individual risk (MTO, Diez Roux), and the geography of redlining still predicts preterm birth and infant mortality today (JAYAKUMAR). The highest-leverage interventions are Housing First models - which consistently reduce mortality and hospitalisation by giving people stable housing before requiring compliance with treatment - and neighbourhood-level investment that improves the physical and social environment for everyone in the area, not just direct program participants. Income and wealth are the underlying mechanism: each $10,000 increase in annual income adds roughly one year of life (Chetty), and income security reduces hospitalisations directly (Mincome). The international comparison with Vienna shows that cities with large-scale social housing prevent the concentrated disinvestment that US cities like Chicago have produced through market-only housing policy.
