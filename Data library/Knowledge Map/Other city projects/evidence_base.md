# Evidence Base: Other City Projects

Gap estimates in action_list.md are derived from the following studies.

---

**Muthukumar P / Branas CC et al. — Citywide cluster-randomized trial to restore blighted vacant land (PNAS)**
Greening and cleaning vacant lots produced a 29% reduction in gun violence in intervention areas. If scaled citywide, the authors estimate over 350 fewer shootings per year in Philadelphia. In Englewood, where homicide is the single largest contributor to premature mortality among young adults, equivalent reductions translate directly to life expectancy gains. Supports ~0.5 yr estimate for vacant lot restoration.

**Unknown Author / Sara Heller — Summer Jobs Reduce Violence Among Disadvantaged Youth**
A randomized controlled trial in Chicago found that summer employment programs reduced violent crime involvement by 43% among participating youth, with effects persisting after the program ended. Supports ~0.6 yr estimate for youth jobs programs.

**Braga AA & Weisburd D — Effects of Focused Deterrence Strategies on Crime (systematic review)**
Meta-analysis of Group Violence Intervention / focused deterrence programs found statistically significant reductions in homicide and violent crime across multiple US cities. Average reductions in homicide of 30–60% in targeted areas. Supports ~1.0 yr estimate for GVI deployment.

**Ransford et al. — Systematic Review on the Effectiveness of the Cure Violence Approach**
Multiple independent evaluations of Cure Violence interventions found shooting reductions of 30–60% in target areas when the model was implemented with fidelity. Baltimore evaluation found 56% reduction in homicides in one district. Supports ~1.5 yr estimate.

**Grey et al. — Evidence on Ceasefire and Group Violence Intervention (2025)**
Updated review confirmed sustained violence reduction effects from community-based interruption models in multiple US cities. Combined GVI + outreach programs showed strongest effects.

**Lisa Barao — Oakland Ceasefire Evaluation (2019)**
Oakland Ceasefire produced a 30% reduction in homicides over three years. Confirms GVI scalability across city contexts similar to Chicago.

**Unknown Author — READI Chicago (Oct 2023)**
READI (Rapid Employment and Development Initiative) combined 18 months of transitional employment with cognitive behavioral therapy for highest-risk men. Participants showed 79% reduction in violent crime involvement compared to controls. Particularly powerful because it targets the individuals most likely to be killed or incarcerated — the primary driver of life expectancy loss in Englewood. Supports ~1.5 yr estimate.

**Walley AY et al. — Opioid Overdose Rates and Naloxone Distribution, Massachusetts**
A Massachusetts naloxone distribution program was associated with a 46% lower opioid overdose mortality rate in communities with high program implementation. Supports ~0.7 yr estimate for naloxone saturation.

**chicago_life_expectancy_foundation.md — Violence reduction bundle estimate**
The project's own synthesis estimates a violence reduction + trauma prevention bundle produces 0.8–1.8 years of community-level life expectancy gain over 5–10 years in the hardest-hit neighborhoods.
