# Role: Network Development Coordinator

## Purpose
Systematically expand IMAN's strategic network — funders, partner organizations, community leaders, and corporate allies — by managing a structured outreach pipeline that converts cold prospects into active, long-term relationships.

---

## Context
IMAN operates at the intersection of faith, health, and social justice in Chicago's South and West sides. This role serves as the connective tissue between IMAN's mission-driven programs and the external ecosystem of people and institutions that can amplify that work through funding, partnership, referrals, advocacy, or in-kind support.

---

## Key Responsibilities

### 1. Prospect Pipeline Management
- Maintain and expand the prospect database (Apollo or equivalent CRM)
- Segment contacts by type: **Funder**, **Partner Organization**, **Community Leader**, **Corporate**, **Government**, **Academic**
- Prioritize outreach queue based on strategic fit, funding cycles, and relationship warmth

### 2. Research & Personalization
- Use Claude Code and web research to build prospect profiles: mission alignment, past giving, current priorities, board connections, shared geography
- Craft personalized outreach that leads with IMAN's impact data and the specific intersection with the prospect's interests
- Avoid generic templates — every first contact should reference something specific

### 3. Outreach Execution
- Send initial emails and LinkedIn messages via Apollo sequences
- Follow up systematically (3-touch cadence over 3–4 weeks)
- Track all activity in CRM with notes on tone, response, and next steps

### 4. Relationship Qualification
- Conduct 30-minute intro calls to establish mutual interest and identify opportunity type
- Document alignment assessment: program fit, funding interest, timeline, decision-makers
- Route qualified relationships to the appropriate IMAN program lead or executive

### 5. Relationship Formalization
- **Funders**: Coordinate with program staff to prepare LOIs, grant proposals, or pitch deck deliveries
- **Partners**: Draft Memoranda of Understanding (MOUs) or partnership agreements
- **Community leaders / advisors**: Onboard to IMAN's advisory network or community coalition
- **Corporate**: Identify volunteer, sponsorship, or in-kind opportunities

### 6. Relationship Nurture
- Schedule recurring touchpoints (quarterly minimum) for active relationships
- Share relevant IMAN updates, impact reports, and program milestones
- Flag warm funders during grant cycles for executive follow-up

### 7. Knowledge Base Maintenance
- Update IMAN's Knowledge Map with new evidence, contacts, and partnership intelligence
- Log key relationship outcomes in the Data Library for institutional memory
- Collaborate with Claude Code to generate reports, update action lists, and produce outreach materials

## Ideal Candidate Profile
- 2–4 years in nonprofit development, partnerships, or community organizing
- Strong written communication — can write with warmth, specificity, and urgency
- Comfortable with CRM tools and systematic outreach workflows
- Genuine alignment with IMAN's values: faith-rooted, justice-oriented, community-centered
- Chicago connections preferred — especially on the South and West sides
- Experience with grant writing or funder relations is a plus, not a requirement
- Comfortable working alongside AI tools (Claude Code) as a research and drafting partner
