# /pitch-deck Skill

## Trigger
When user types `/pitch-deck` or asks to "generate a pitch deck" or "create a pitch deck" for a project idea.

## Purpose
Generate a structured pitch deck outline for a new IMAN initiative, following the same slide architecture as the Green ReEntry Deck (2024). The output is a slide-by-slide content document that can be handed to a designer or imported into a presentation tool.

## Instructions for Claude

When this skill is triggered, ask the user for the following inputs if not already provided:
- **Project name** — What is this initiative called?
- **Target population** — Who does it serve?
- **Core services** — What are the 2–4 main program components?
- **Geography** — Where will it operate?
- **Key outcomes / theory of change** — What changes for participants and why?

Then generate the pitch deck using the slide structure below. Pull in relevant evidence, statistics, and context from the IMAN Knowledge Map (in `Data library/Knowledge Map/`) and IMAN's existing programs when applicable. Write each slide as if it will be read by a funder or community partner encountering the program for the first time.

## Slide Structure

### SLIDE 1 — COVER
**Elements:**
- Program name (large, bold)
- Tagline (1 sentence: what it does and for whom)
- Geography (e.g., "Chicago, Atlanta & Beyond" or specific neighborhood)
- IMAN logo + program logo/icon if applicable

**Tone:** Bold, aspirational. This is the first impression.

---

### SLIDE 2 — MISSION STATEMENT
**Elements:**
- 1–2 sentence mission statement in large text:
  "[Program name] provides [core services] for [target population], as a holistic approach to [broader community impact — e.g., neighborhood stabilization, violence prevention, health equity]."
- Highlight key phrases in bold (target population and community impact words)

**Tone:** Clear and human. Avoid jargon. Make the reader feel the stakes immediately.

---

### SLIDE 3 — PROGRAM COMPONENTS OVERVIEW
**Elements:**
- 3 main program pillars (top row), each with:
  - Icon
  - Pillar name (bold, large)
  - 2–3 sentence description
  - Format tag (e.g., "ONE YEAR | WEEKDAYS", "12–18 MONTH RESIDENCE")
- 3 support services (bottom row), each with:
  - Service name (in accent color)
  - 2–3 sentence description

**Guidance:** The top row should be the core programming (skills training, direct service, housing, etc.). The bottom row should be wraparound supports (legal, job placement, alumni/follow-up). Mirror the Green ReEntry structure: big program + wraparound.

---

### SLIDE 4 — THEORY OF CHANGE
**Elements:**
- A bold, humanizing headline (e.g., "Everyone wants to succeed, but not everyone knows how.")
- A paragraph in the form: "If [target population] are provided [key inputs: hard skills, soft skills, housing stability, healthcare, etc.], then they will be on a thriving pathway to [financial / spiritual / social-emotional / physical] growth that will impact themselves, their families, and their communities."
- Photo of participants in action (right side)

**Tone:** Dignified. Centers participant agency and potential, not deficits.

---

### SLIDE 5 — A HOLISTIC APPROACH
**Elements:**
- "A Holistic Approach" label (vertical, left side)
- 3 key program integrations listed with bold subheadings and 1-sentence descriptions:
  1. Mental health / therapy integration (e.g., "One-on-one and group therapy sessions for every participant — X hours per week")
  2. Wellness, financial literacy, and behavioral health (e.g., "Weekly sessions gaining more than just hard skills and new careers")
  3. Integration with Arts & Culture, Health Center, and Organizing (e.g., "Providing all participants with a full, holistic curriculum")
- Circular ecosystem graphic (right side) showing interconnected domains: Advocacy, Housing, Jobs, Therapy, Arts/Culture, Workforce Development

**Tone:** Systemic. Shows this is not a single-service program — it's a full ecosystem approach.

---

### SLIDE 6 — CREDENTIALS & ACCREDITATION
**Elements:**
- Bold headline: "Paid, [adjective]-accredited, and industry-certified programs."
- Subheading: "[Number] accredited certifications offered"
- Bullet list of program tracks with accreditation partner:
  - [Program track 1]: [Accreditation body / college partner]
  - [Program track 2]: [Accreditation body / college partner]
- Photo of participant doing hands-on work

**Guidance:** Emphasize that participants are paid, credentialed, and connected to continuing education. This differentiates IMAN from charity models.

---

### SLIDE 7 — COMPONENT DEEP-DIVE #1 (Primary Program Pillar)
**Elements:**
- Large heading: [Pillar Name]
- 3 bullet points with bold lead-ins showing key stats or outcomes:
  - [Metric 1]
  - [Metric 2]
  - [Metric 3]
- Sub-label for any sub-services provided within this pillar
- Photo (right side)

**Guidance:** Use real data if available from the Knowledge Map or IMAN's existing programs. If projecting, label as "projected" and cite the evidence base.

---

### SLIDE 8 — COMPONENT DEEP-DIVE #2 (Job Placement / Economic Outcomes)
**Elements:**
- Large heading: "Job Placement" or "[Economic Outcome]"
- 2–3 bullet points with quantified outcomes
- "SUCCESS STORY" callout box: one concrete graduate/participant story (name optional, outcome specific)
- Photo of graduation or workforce milestone

**Guidance:** Funders respond to economic mobility outcomes. Lead with placement rates, wage levels, union access, or education continuation.

---

### SLIDE 9 — COMPONENT DEEP-DIVE #3 (Legal / Structural Barrier Removal)
**Elements:**
- Large heading: "Legal Services" or "[Barrier Removal Component]"
- 3 bullet points on legal or structural barrier services:
  - [Service type + # of participants served]
  - [Partner organizations / clinic types]
  - [Education/awareness component]
- Photo of advocacy or civic engagement

**Guidance:** This slide shows that the program understands systemic barriers — not just skills gaps — prevent economic mobility. Expungement, housing law, benefits access, etc.

---

### SLIDE 10 — A FOCUS ON HEALING
**Elements:**
- Large heading: "A Focus on Healing"
- Paragraph: "As part of IMAN's holistic model, all participants receive regular primary and behavioral health care and support:"
- 2 bullet points showing health integration stats:
  - Behavioral health team touchpoints / patients served
  - Community group types offered (trauma recovery, grief, parenting, etc.)
- Photo of participants with care team

**Guidance:** This slide connects the new initiative to IMAN's Health Center. Every IMAN program routes participants into the health ecosystem.

---

### SLIDE 11 — HIGHLIGHTS (YEAR–YEAR)
**Elements:**
- Large heading: "[Year]–[Year] HIGHLIGHTS" with bold vertical bar accent
- 3 bullet points with underlined key numbers:
  - Total participants served since launch
  - Demand indicator (e.g., applicants vs. cohort size, waitlist)
  - Completion/graduation rate or retention metric
- Photo of graduation or cohort event (right side)

**Guidance:** Use actuals if available. If this is a new program, use projections from comparable IMAN programs or evidence-based benchmarks from the Knowledge Map.

---

### SLIDE 12 — BUILDING THE FUTURE
**Elements:**
- "Building the Future" label (vertical, left side)
- 3 category sections, each with a labeled header box and 2 expansion items:
  - **[Infrastructure Category]** (e.g., Housing, Facility, Equipment)
    - [Expansion item 1]: [Brief description]
    - [Expansion item 2]: [Brief description]
  - **[Program Category]** (e.g., Trades, Curriculum, Cohort Size)
    - [Expansion item 1]: [Brief description]
    - [Expansion item 2]: [Brief description]
  - **[Graduate Support Category]** (e.g., Alumni, Placement, Mentorship)
    - [Expansion item 1]: [Brief description]
- Photo of ribbon cutting or expansion milestone (right side)

**Tone:** Forward-looking. Shows funders their investment has a roadmap beyond current operations.

---

### SLIDE 13 — IN THE NEWS
**Elements:**
- Heading: "IN THE NEWS:"
- 3–4 press mentions with outlet logo/name and headline:
  - [Outlet]: "[Headline]"
  - [Outlet]: "[Headline]"
  - [Outlet]: "[Headline]"
- 1–2 embedded video thumbnails (right side) linking to media coverage
- Accent color background

**Guidance:** If this is a new program with no press yet, substitute with: quotes from community members, letters of support, or adjacent IMAN program press. Flag clearly if projected.

---

### SLIDE 14 — ABOUT IMAN
**Elements:**
- Large heading: "ABOUT IMAN:"
- Subheading: "FOSTERING HEALTH, WELLNESS, AND HEALING"
- 3-paragraph description:
  - Para 1: IMAN's mission and core methods (health center, arts, organizing)
  - Para 2: Founding (1997), South Side Chicago roots, Atlanta expansion (2016)
  - Para 3: IMAN's integrative model — primary and behavioral health, artistic expression, leadership development, organizing, housing, job training — and its goal to increase quality of life for people in marginalized communities.
- Photo of community action or protest (right side with green overlay)

**Note:** This slide uses IMAN's standard boilerplate language. Do not customize it per project — it is a consistent org identifier.

---

## Output Format

Generate the pitch deck as a .pptx document with:
- Each slide as a `## Slide [N] — [Title]` section
- Content written as ready-to-use copy (not as instructions)
- Placeholders in [brackets] where real data, photos, or project-specific details are needed
- A "Designer Notes" line at the bottom of each slide for layout/visual guidance
- A summary table at the top listing: Program Name, Target Population, Geography, # of Slides, Key Ask (funding amount or partnership type if known)
- Read and follow the skill at "./Data library/skills/frontend-slides/SKILL.md" to design the slides

## Constraints
- Write in IMAN's voice: dignified, community-centered, bold, faith-informed but secular-serving
- Do not invent statistics — use data from the Knowledge Map or label estimates as "projected" with a source
- Always connect the new program to at least one existing IMAN program or infrastructure asset (health center, Fresh Market, Green ReEntry, Griot Plaza)
- Keep each slide to the content density shown in the Green ReEntry Deck: short, punchy, large type — not dense paragraphs
