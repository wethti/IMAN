# Housing & Community Building — Folder Summary

Housing instability is one of the strongest independent predictors of premature mortality. In Englewood and Chicago Lawn, decades of disinvestment, redlining, and school closings have produced concentrated poverty, vacant structures, and a near-complete absence of market-rate housing investment. This folder covers the evidence base and intervention options for reversing that.

**What the research shows:** Housing First models consistently reduce hospitalizations and mortality among previously unstable populations. Children who move to lower-poverty neighborhoods show measurable improvements in health, earnings, and longevity (Chetty MTO). Residential segregation is itself a cause of death — not just a background condition. Place-based community development (anchor institutions, public space, transit) compounds the effect of housing by changing the entire environment rather than just one household at a time.

**What IMAN is already doing:** IMAN operates 9 transitional housing units (rehabbed through Green ReEntry), is nearing ground-break on the Regenerator ($20M+ permanent supportive housing hub in a former school), and anchors the GoGreen Unreseen corridor with the Fresh Market, Griot Plaza, and planned community spaces. Over $40M in catalytic investment has flowed into the 63rd & Racine corridor. A dedicated women's housing unit is planned. The Greenlight the Green Line campaign secured a mandate to reopen the Racine CTA station by 2029.

**Gap:** IMAN's housing capacity remains small relative to neighborhood need. The Regenerator, once complete, will be the first large-scale permanent supportive housing project. Anti-displacement tools (community land trust, inclusionary zoning) are not yet part of the portfolio. Environmental remediation of vacant lots remains in planning.

**Estimated life expectancy contribution of full portfolio:** 3–5 years of gap reduction over 10–15 years, primarily through permanent supportive housing, neighborhood corridor investment, and transit access.
